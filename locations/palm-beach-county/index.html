Addiction Treatment in Palm Beach County, Florida

Palm Beach County is the largest county by area in Florida and home to more than 1.5 million residents spanning dozens of cities and unincorporated communities. From West Palm Beach and Jupiter in the north to Boca Raton and Delray Beach in the south, the county's diverse population includes many individuals and families affected by substance use disorders, opioid addiction, alcohol dependence, and co-occurring mental health conditions. Campus Recovery Center, centrally located in Lake Worth Beach, serves as a trusted outpatient treatment provider for communities across Palm Beach County.

Our facility at 19 S Dixie Hwy is strategically positioned in the center of the county, providing convenient access via I-95, the Florida Turnpike, and US-1 from virtually any Palm Beach County community. Whether you live in Royal Palm Beach, Wellington, Palm Beach Gardens, Greenacres, or Lantana, Campus Recovery Center is within a reasonable driving distance and offers the clinical depth and accreditation standards you should expect from a drug rehab in Palm Beach County.

Why Palm Beach County Residents Choose Campus Recovery

Palm Beach County has a well-known concentration of addiction treatment providers, but not all programs are created equal. Campus Recovery Center distinguishes itself through several key strengths:

  • Central county location: Our Lake Worth Beach facility is accessible from across Palm Beach County, situated along the I-95 and US-1 corridors with ample parking and public transit access.
  • Joint Commission accredited: We hold the Gold Seal of Approval from the Joint Commission, the most respected accreditation body in health care. This ensures our programs meet rigorous national standards for quality, safety, and clinical outcomes.
  • Full continuum of care: PHP, IOP, and OP levels allow clients to step down through decreasing levels of intensity as their recovery strengthens, all within the same treatment team.
  • Dual-diagnosis treatment: We specialize in treating substance use disorders alongside mental health conditions such as depression, anxiety, PTSD, bipolar disorder, and schizoaffective disorder.
  • Medication-assisted treatment: Vivitrol, Naltrexone, and Suboxone are available for clients with opioid or alcohol use disorders when medically indicated.
  • Insurance accepted: We work with most major insurance carriers and provide free, confidential benefits verification.

Our Treatment Programs Serving Palm Beach County

Campus Recovery Center offers three levels of outpatient addiction and mental health treatment, providing a comprehensive continuum for Palm Beach County residents:

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

Our PHP is the most intensive outpatient level, offering five hours of clinical programming seven days a week. This program includes individual therapy, group counseling, psychoeducation, psychiatric evaluations, medication management, and case management. PHP is ideal for individuals stepping down from detox or residential treatment, or for those who need a high level of daily structure to begin their recovery.

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

The IOP provides three hours of daily treatment, starting at six days per week and decreasing as clinically appropriate. This level allows Palm Beach County clients to maintain work, school, or family obligations while continuing meaningful therapeutic engagement. IOP includes all core clinical services and serves as a bridge between PHP and independent recovery.

Outpatient Program (OP)

Our OP offers two sessions per week for clients who have achieved stability and are transitioning toward self-directed recovery. Services include weekly individual counseling, group therapy, and ongoing psychiatric care. Aftercare planning ensures that Palm Beach County clients leave our program with a sustainable support plan in place.

Palm Beach County Recovery Resources

Palm Beach County offers an extensive infrastructure of recovery support resources. The Southeast Florida Behavioral Health Network coordinates publicly funded treatment services across the county. The Palm Beach County Substance Awareness Coalition works on prevention and education initiatives. Numerous AA, NA, and SMART Recovery meetings are held daily throughout the county. The county's crisis stabilization units, including those at JFK Medical Center and St. Mary's Medical Center, provide acute care for individuals who need stabilization before transitioning to outpatient treatment at Campus Recovery Center.
