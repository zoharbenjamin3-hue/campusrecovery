Humana Insurance for Addiction & Mental Health Treatment

Author: Daniel Touloumis
Editor: Devin Scheller, Director of Operations
Medical Reviewer: Sahil Talwar, PA
Published: August 1, 2024
Last Edited: March 29, 2026

Campus Recovery Center accepts Humana insurance for addiction and mental health treatment at our Lake Worth Beach, Florida facility. As an in-network Humana provider, we work directly with Humana to verify your behavioral health benefits and handle prior authorization, so you can focus on recovery without the burden of navigating insurance alone.

Humana Behavioral Health Benefits

Humana provides behavioral health coverage through its medical plans, covering both substance use disorder and mental health conditions. Under the Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA) and the Affordable Care Act (ACA), Humana is required to cover behavioral health treatment at levels comparable to medical and surgical care.

Humana behavioral health benefits typically include:

  • Substance use disorder assessment and diagnosis
  • Individual and group therapy for addiction and mental health
  • Psychiatric evaluation and medication management
  • Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) including Suboxone and Vivitrol
  • Crisis intervention and stabilization services
  • Dual diagnosis treatment for co-occurring disorders

Humana Plans We Accept

Campus Recovery Center accepts most Humana plan types for addiction and mental health treatment. Coverage levels, copays, and deductibles vary by plan, but our admissions team verifies your specific benefits before treatment begins.

Humana PPO Plans

Preferred Provider Organization plans offer the greatest flexibility. You can receive treatment at Campus Recovery Center as an in-network provider with lower out-of-pocket costs, no referral needed for behavioral health services.

Humana HMO Plans

Health Maintenance Organization plans may require a referral from your primary care physician for behavioral health treatment. Our admissions team coordinates with your PCP and Humana to secure the necessary authorizations.

Humana EPO Plans

Exclusive Provider Organization plans cover in-network services without requiring referrals. As a Humana network provider, Campus Recovery Center provides covered treatment under EPO plans.

Humana Medicare Advantage

Humana Medicare Advantage (Part C) plans include behavioral health benefits covering substance use disorder and mental health treatment. Coverage mirrors Original Medicare with potential additional benefits depending on your specific Humana MA plan.

Levels of Care Covered by Humana

Humana covers multiple levels of outpatient behavioral health treatment at Campus Recovery Center. The appropriate level of care is determined through clinical assessment and must meet medical necessity criteria established by Humana.

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

PHP is the most intensive outpatient level of care, providing structured treatment five to seven days per week for approximately six hours per day. Humana typically covers PHP for individuals stepping down from residential treatment or those who require intensive clinical support but do not need 24-hour supervision. PHP at Campus Recovery Center includes individual therapy, group counseling, psychiatric services, and evidence-based programming for addiction and mental health.

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

IOP provides structured treatment three to five days per week for approximately three hours per session. Humana covers IOP as a step-down from PHP or as a primary treatment option for individuals with moderate clinical needs. Our IOP includes individual therapy, process groups, psychoeducation, relapse prevention planning, and family programming.

Outpatient Treatment (OP)

Standard outpatient treatment includes individual therapy sessions, psychiatric appointments, and ongoing medication management. Humana covers outpatient behavioral health visits with applicable copays. Outpatient treatment is appropriate for individuals transitioning from IOP or those with lower clinical acuity who benefit from regular therapeutic support.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

Humana covers Medication-Assisted Treatment for opioid and alcohol use disorders. MAT combines FDA-approved medications—such as buprenorphine (Suboxone), naltrexone (Vivitrol), and acamprosate—with behavioral therapy and counseling. Campus Recovery Center provides comprehensive MAT services integrated into all levels of care.

Humana Prior Authorization Process

Most Humana plans require prior authorization for PHP and IOP levels of care. Campus Recovery Center manages the entire authorization process:

  1. Contact Our Admissions Team

    Call (877) 995-0102 with your Humana insurance card. We collect your member ID, group number, and plan details.

  2. Benefits Verification

    We contact Humana directly to verify your behavioral health benefits, including covered services, copays, deductible status, out-of-pocket maximums, and any plan-specific requirements.

  3. Prior Authorization Submission

    Our clinical team submits the prior authorization request to Humana with supporting clinical documentation. We advocate for the appropriate level of care based on your assessment.

  4. Begin Treatment

    Once authorization is secured, you can begin treatment with a clear understanding of your coverage and any financial responsibility.

Understanding Your Humana Costs

Your out-of-pocket costs for addiction and mental health treatment under Humana depend on your specific plan. Key cost factors include:

  • Deductible: The amount you pay before Humana begins covering services. If you have already met your annual deductible, your share of treatment costs may be lower.
  • Copay or coinsurance: Your share of each covered service after the deductible is met. Copays are a fixed amount per visit; coinsurance is a percentage of the allowed amount.
  • Out-of-pocket maximum: The most you will pay in a plan year for covered services. Once reached, Humana covers 100% of allowed charges for the remainder of the year.
  • In-network vs. out-of-network: Receiving treatment at an in-network facility like Campus Recovery Center typically results in significantly lower costs than out-of-network care.

Humana Coverage for Dual Diagnosis Treatment

Many individuals struggling with addiction also have co-occurring mental health conditions such as depression, anxiety, PTSD, or bipolar disorder. Humana behavioral health benefits cover integrated dual diagnosis treatment, which addresses both conditions simultaneously. Campus Recovery Center specializes in dual diagnosis care, providing psychiatric evaluation, medication management, and evidence-based therapies tailored to co-occurring disorders.

Frequently Asked Questions

Yes. Humana covers substance use disorder treatment including partial hospitalization (PHP), intensive outpatient (IOP), outpatient therapy, and medication-assisted treatment (MAT) under its behavioral health benefits. Coverage levels and out-of-pocket costs depend on your specific Humana plan.

Does Humana require prior authorization for rehab?

Most Humana plans require prior authorization for PHP and IOP levels of care. Campus Recovery Center handles the entire authorization process on your behalf, working directly with Humana to secure approval before treatment begins.

What Humana plans does Campus Recovery Center accept?

We accept most Humana plan types including Humana PPO, HMO, EPO, Humana Gold Plus, Humana Medicare Advantage, and employer-sponsored Humana plans. Call (877) 995-0102 to verify your specific plan.

How do I verify my Humana benefits for mental health treatment?

Call our admissions team at (877) 995-0102. We contact Humana directly to verify your behavioral health benefits, including coverage for all levels of care, copays, deductibles, and out-of-pocket maximums. Verification is free and confidential.
