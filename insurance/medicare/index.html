Medicare provides coverage for outpatient addiction and mental health treatment at Campus Recovery Center in Lake Worth Beach, Florida. Whether you have Original Medicare (Part B) or a Medicare Advantage plan, you can access evidence-based behavioral health services to support your recovery.

How Medicare Covers Behavioral Health Services

Medicare recognizes substance use disorders and mental health conditions as medical conditions that require professional treatment. Under Medicare Part B (Medical Insurance), beneficiaries have access to outpatient behavioral health services provided by Medicare-enrolled facilities and practitioners.

The Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA) applies to Medicare Advantage plans, ensuring that behavioral health coverage is comparable to medical and surgical benefits. Original Medicare has also expanded its behavioral health coverage significantly, removing barriers such as lifetime day limits on psychiatric treatment.

Medicare Part B Outpatient Behavioral Health Coverage

Medicare Part B covers outpatient mental health and substance use disorder treatment services when provided by Medicare-participating providers. Campus Recovery Center is enrolled as a Medicare provider and offers the following covered services:

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

Medicare Part B covers partial hospitalization as an alternative to inpatient psychiatric care. PHP provides structured clinical programming for 20 or more hours per week, including therapy, psychiatric evaluation, and skill-building. PHP must be provided by a Medicare-certified program and requires a physician certification of medical necessity.

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

Medicare covers intensive outpatient services for substance use disorders and mental health conditions. IOP provides 9 to 19 hours of structured clinical programming per week, including group therapy, individual counseling, and relapse prevention. Medicare began covering IOP as a distinct benefit category to improve access to stepped-down care.

Outpatient Therapy & Counseling

Medicare Part B covers individual psychotherapy, group therapy, family counseling, and diagnostic evaluations for behavioral health conditions. Services must be provided by or under the supervision of a licensed practitioner enrolled in Medicare.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

Medicare covers MAT for opioid and alcohol use disorders. Medicare Part B covers the office visits and counseling, while Medicare Part D or Medicare Advantage plans cover the prescription medications such as buprenorphine (Suboxone) and naltrexone (Vivitrol).

Additional Medicare-Covered Behavioral Health Services

  • Comprehensive psychiatric diagnostic evaluations
  • Individual psychotherapy (30, 45, or 60 minutes)
  • Group psychotherapy and psychoeducation
  • Psychiatric medication management and evaluation
  • Crisis intervention services
  • Family therapy when clinically indicated
  • Screening, Brief Intervention, and Referral to Treatment (SBIRT)
  • Annual depression screening at no cost as a preventive benefit
  • Annual alcohol misuse screening and up to four brief counseling sessions at no cost
  • Opioid use disorder treatment services including counseling and drug testing

Understanding Medicare Costs for Behavioral Health

Under Original Medicare (Part B), your out-of-pocket costs for outpatient behavioral health services are:

  • Annual deductible: You pay the Part B annual deductible before Medicare begins paying its share.
  • Coinsurance: After meeting your deductible, Medicare pays 80% of the Medicare-approved amount. You are responsible for the remaining 20% coinsurance.
  • Medigap: If you have a Medicare Supplement (Medigap) plan, it may cover some or all of your 20% coinsurance, significantly reducing your out-of-pocket costs.

Certain preventive behavioral health services, including annual depression and alcohol screenings, are covered at 100% with no cost-sharing when provided by a participating provider.

Medicare Advantage Plans (Part C)

Medicare Advantage plans are offered by private insurance companies approved by Medicare. These plans must cover everything that Original Medicare covers, but many offer additional behavioral health benefits such as:

  • Lower copays for outpatient behavioral health visits
  • Broader provider networks for substance use disorder treatment
  • Integrated prescription drug coverage (Part D) for MAT medications
  • Care coordination and case management services
  • Telehealth options for therapy and psychiatric consultations

Campus Recovery Center works with many Medicare Advantage plans available in South Florida. Contact our admissions team to verify whether your specific Medicare Advantage plan is accepted.

Medicare and Dual Diagnosis Treatment

Many Medicare beneficiaries seeking addiction treatment also have co-occurring mental health conditions such as depression, anxiety, post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), or chronic pain. Medicare covers integrated treatment for co-occurring disorders, which means you can receive coordinated care for both substance use and mental health conditions within the same program.

Campus Recovery Center provides dual diagnosis treatment designed to address the complex relationship between addiction and mental health. Our clinical team develops individualized treatment plans that incorporate evidence-based therapies for both conditions simultaneously.

Medicare Coverage for Older Adults in Recovery

Substance use disorders and mental health conditions affect people of all ages. Older adults face unique challenges including prescription medication misuse, late-onset alcohol use disorder, grief-related depression, and the intersection of chronic health conditions with behavioral health needs. Medicare benefits are designed to support treatment that addresses these age-specific clinical considerations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Yes. Medicare Part B covers outpatient substance use disorder treatment including individual and group therapy, psychiatric services, partial hospitalization programs, intensive outpatient programs, and medication-assisted treatment when provided by Medicare-enrolled providers.

What is the difference between Medicare Part B and Medicare Advantage for behavioral health?

Original Medicare Part B covers outpatient behavioral health services at 80% after your annual deductible. Medicare Advantage (Part C) plans must cover everything Original Medicare covers but may offer additional benefits, lower copays, or broader provider networks depending on the plan. Contact us to verify your specific plan’s coverage.

Does Medicare cover Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)?

Yes. Medicare covers MAT for opioid and alcohol use disorders. Part B covers the office visits and counseling component, while Part D or your Medicare Advantage plan covers the medications such as Suboxone and Vivitrol.

How much does Medicare pay for outpatient addiction treatment?

Under Original Medicare Part B, Medicare pays 80% of the approved amount for outpatient behavioral health services after your annual deductible. You pay the remaining 20% coinsurance. A Medigap supplemental plan can help cover this cost. Some preventive screenings are covered at 100% with no cost-sharing.

Medicare is the federal health insurance program primarily for individuals aged 65 and older, as well as certain younger individuals with disabilities or specific medical conditions. Medicare provides behavioral health benefits through Part A (hospital insurance) and Part B (medical insurance) that cover substance use disorder and mental health treatment.

Medicare Behavioral Health Coverage

Medicare provides comprehensive behavioral health benefits for substance use disorder and mental health treatment. Medicare Part A covers partial hospitalization programs when provided by a Medicare-certified facility. Medicare Part B covers outpatient behavioral health services including individual and group therapy, psychiatric evaluation, medication management, and intensive outpatient programs.

Medicare has expanded its behavioral health coverage in recent years, removing lifetime limits on inpatient psychiatric care and eliminating higher cost-sharing for outpatient mental health services. Medicare beneficiaries now pay the same coinsurance rate for behavioral health services as they do for other Part B medical services.

Medicare Coverage Types

Campus Recovery Center works with Medicare beneficiaries across multiple coverage types, including:

  • Original Medicare (Part A & Part B) – Traditional fee-for-service Medicare with behavioral health coverage under both Part A and Part B
  • Medicare Advantage (Part C) – Private insurance plans that include all Part A and Part B benefits plus often additional behavioral health services
  • Medicare with Medigap (Supplement) – Original Medicare paired with a supplemental plan that helps cover deductibles, coinsurance, and copays for behavioral health services
  • Medicare-Medicaid Dual Eligible – Beneficiaries enrolled in both Medicare and Medicaid with coordinated behavioral health benefits

Coverage details, deductibles, coinsurance, and copays vary by coverage type. Our admissions team verifies your specific Medicare benefits before treatment begins.

Treatment Programs Covered by Medicare

Medicare behavioral health benefits typically cover the following levels of care at Campus Recovery Center:

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

Structured programming five to seven days per week for individuals who need intensive clinical support. PHP includes individual therapy, group counseling, psychiatric evaluation, and medication management. Medicare Part A covers PHP at Medicare-certified facilities when medical necessity criteria are met.

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

Flexible programming three to five days per week, allowing clients to maintain responsibilities while receiving treatment. IOP includes evidence-based therapies, relapse prevention, and clinical support. Medicare Part B covers IOP for both substance use and mental health conditions.

Outpatient Treatment (OP)

Individual counseling, group therapy, and psychiatric services on a less intensive schedule. Outpatient treatment serves as a step-down from IOP or as standalone care. Medicare Part B covers outpatient behavioral health visits with the standard 20% coinsurance after the annual deductible is met.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

FDA-approved medications combined with counseling and behavioral therapies for opioid and alcohol use disorders. Medicare covers MAT medications including buprenorphine, naltrexone, and other prescribed treatments. Medicare Part B also covers opioid treatment program services.

Dual Diagnosis Treatment

Integrated care for individuals with both a substance use disorder and a co-occurring mental health condition such as depression, anxiety, PTSD, or bipolar disorder. Medicare behavioral health benefits cover dual diagnosis treatment when clinical criteria are satisfied.

Medicare Part A vs. Part B for Behavioral Health

Medicare Part A covers partial hospitalization programs (PHP) at Medicare-certified facilities. Part A has an annual deductible and covers PHP services after the deductible is met. PHP under Part A is designed for individuals who need intensive structured treatment but do not require 24-hour inpatient care.

Medicare Part B covers outpatient behavioral health services including individual and group therapy, psychiatric evaluation, medication management, intensive outpatient programs, and medication-assisted treatment. After the annual Part B deductible is met, Medicare typically pays 80% of the Medicare-approved amount, and the beneficiary is responsible for the remaining 20% coinsurance.

Beneficiaries with Medigap supplemental insurance or Medicare Advantage plans may have reduced or eliminated out-of-pocket costs for behavioral health services.
