Molina Healthcare Coverage for Addiction & Mental Health Treatment

Campus Recovery Center accepts Molina Healthcare for addiction and mental health treatment at our Lake Worth Beach, Florida facility. Molina Medicaid and marketplace plans provide behavioral health coverage for eligible members.

Campus Recovery Center accepts Molina Healthcare for addiction and mental health treatment at our Lake Worth Beach, Florida facility. Molina Healthcare is a major managed care organization that provides health coverage through Medicaid managed care plans and Health Insurance Marketplace plans. Molina serves millions of members across the United States and provides comprehensive behavioral health benefits for substance use disorder and mental health treatment.

Molina Healthcare Behavioral Health Coverage

Molina Healthcare plans include behavioral health benefits for substance use disorder treatment and mental health services. Molina operates as both a Medicaid managed care organization and a marketplace insurer in Florida, and both plan types cover behavioral health treatment.

Molina Medicaid managed care plans provide behavioral health coverage as part of Florida’s Statewide Medicaid Managed Care program. Molina marketplace plans are ACA-compliant and are required under the Affordable Care Act and the Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA) to cover behavioral health treatment as an essential health benefit at levels comparable to medical and surgical care.

Molina Healthcare Plans Accepted

Campus Recovery Center works with Molina Healthcare plan types, including:

  • Molina Medicaid Managed Care – Florida Medicaid plans administered by Molina Healthcare with behavioral health benefits for eligible Medicaid beneficiaries
  • Molina Marketplace Bronze – Lower monthly premium marketplace plans with behavioral health coverage as an essential health benefit
  • Molina Marketplace Silver – Mid-tier marketplace plans with balanced premiums and out-of-pocket costs; eligible for cost-sharing reductions
  • Molina Marketplace Gold – Higher monthly premium marketplace plans with lower out-of-pocket costs for behavioral health and other services
  • Molina Medicare-Medicaid Plans – Dual eligible plans for individuals enrolled in both Medicare and Medicaid with coordinated behavioral health benefits

Coverage details, copays, deductibles, and out-of-pocket maximums vary by plan type and tier. Our admissions team verifies your specific Molina Healthcare policy before treatment begins.

Treatment Programs Covered by Molina Healthcare

Molina Healthcare behavioral health benefits typically cover the following levels of care at Campus Recovery Center:

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

Structured programming five to seven days per week for individuals who need intensive clinical support. PHP includes individual therapy, group counseling, psychiatric evaluation, and medication management. Molina Healthcare covers PHP when medical necessity criteria are met and prior authorization is obtained.

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

Flexible programming three to five days per week, allowing clients to maintain work, school, or family responsibilities while receiving treatment. IOP includes evidence-based therapies, relapse prevention, and clinical support. Molina Healthcare plans cover IOP for both substance use and mental health conditions.

Outpatient Treatment (OP)

Individual counseling, group therapy, and psychiatric services on a less intensive schedule. Outpatient treatment serves as a step-down from IOP or as standalone care. Molina Healthcare plans cover outpatient behavioral health visits for eligible members.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

FDA-approved medications combined with counseling and behavioral therapies for opioid and alcohol use disorders. Molina Healthcare covers MAT medications including buprenorphine, naltrexone, and other prescribed treatments when part of a comprehensive treatment plan.

Dual Diagnosis Treatment

Integrated care for individuals with both a substance use disorder and a co-occurring mental health condition such as depression, anxiety, PTSD, or bipolar disorder. Molina Healthcare behavioral health benefits cover dual diagnosis treatment when clinical criteria are satisfied.

  2. We Contact Molina Healthcare Directly

  3. Review Your Benefits

    We explain your Molina Healthcare coverage in plain language—what treatment is covered, your copay and deductible amounts (if applicable), your out-of-pocket maximum, and any session or day limits that apply to your plan. Medicaid members typically have little to no out-of-pocket costs.

  4. Begin Treatment

    Once your Molina Healthcare benefits are verified and any required authorizations are in place, our team coordinates the clinical intake process so you can begin treatment as quickly as possible.

Molina Medicaid vs. Molina Marketplace Plans

Molina Medicaid managed care plans are available to Florida residents who qualify for Medicaid based on income and eligibility criteria. Medicaid members enrolled in Molina typically have little to no out-of-pocket costs for behavioral health services. Coverage is managed through Florida’s Statewide Medicaid Managed Care program.

Molina marketplace plans are ACA-compliant plans purchased through the Health Insurance Marketplace (HealthCare.gov). These plans have copays, deductibles, and out-of-pocket maximums that vary by metal tier. Individuals who qualify for premium tax credits or cost-sharing reductions through the marketplace may have reduced costs for treatment.

Both Molina plan types may require prior authorization for certain levels of care such as partial hospitalization. Our admissions team handles the authorization process on your behalf.

What Information Do I Need to Verify Molina Healthcare Coverage?

When you call to verify your Molina Healthcare benefits, have the following information available:

  • Policyholder or beneficiary name and date of birth
  • Patient name and date of birth (if different from the policyholder)
  • Molina Healthcare customer service phone number (on the back of your card)
