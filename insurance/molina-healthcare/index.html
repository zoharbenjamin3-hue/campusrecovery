Molina Healthcare Coverage for Addiction & Mental Health Treatment

Author: Daniel Touloumis
Editor: Devin Scheller, Director of Operations
Medical Reviewer: Sahil Talwar, PA
Published: August 1, 2024
Last Edited: March 29, 2026

Campus Recovery Center accepts Molina Healthcare insurance for addiction and mental health treatment at our Lake Worth Beach, Florida facility. Whether you are covered through Molina's Medicaid managed care plan or a Molina Marketplace plan, behavioral health services are included in your benefits. Our admissions team verifies your coverage at no cost so you can begin treatment with confidence.

About Molina Healthcare

Molina Healthcare is a national managed care company that provides health insurance to individuals and families through government-sponsored programs and the ACA marketplace. In Florida, Molina participates in the Statewide Medicaid Managed Care (SMMC) program and also offers individual and family plans through the Health Insurance Marketplace at healthcare.gov.

Molina serves a large population of Medicaid members in Florida, providing access to medical, behavioral health, pharmacy, and preventive care services. The company also offers marketplace plans with Bronze, Silver, and Gold tiers for individuals who do not qualify for Medicaid but need affordable coverage.

Molina Medicaid Managed Care in Florida

Florida operates its Medicaid program through a managed care model. Molina Healthcare is one of several managed care organizations contracted by the state to administer Medicaid benefits, including behavioral health services. If you are enrolled in Molina's Medicaid plan in Florida, your behavioral health benefits include:

  • Substance use disorder screening, assessment, and diagnosis
  • Individual, group, and family counseling for addiction and mental health
  • Partial Hospitalization Programs (PHP)
  • Intensive Outpatient Programs (IOP)
  • Outpatient therapy and psychiatric services
  • Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) including buprenorphine, naltrexone, and methadone
  • Crisis stabilization and intervention services
  • Peer support and recovery services

Florida Medicaid through Molina typically has minimal or no cost-sharing for behavioral health services. Most Medicaid members pay no copays for substance use disorder treatment, though some services may require a referral or prior authorization.

Molina Marketplace Plans

Molina also offers health insurance plans through the ACA marketplace in Florida. These plans are available to individuals and families who do not qualify for Medicaid and purchase coverage during open enrollment or a qualifying life event. As ACA marketplace plans, Molina Marketplace products must cover substance use disorder and mental health treatment as essential health benefits.

Molina Marketplace plan tiers include:

  • Molina Bronze – Lower premiums with higher deductibles and out-of-pocket costs. Behavioral health services are covered after meeting the annual deductible.
  • Molina Silver – Moderate premiums and cost-sharing. Silver plans may qualify for cost-sharing reductions that lower copays and deductibles for behavioral health visits.
  • Molina Gold – Higher premiums with the lowest deductibles and copays per visit, providing the most predictable costs for ongoing treatment.

Many Molina Marketplace members qualify for premium tax credits and cost-sharing reductions based on household income. These subsidies can significantly reduce the cost of addiction and mental health treatment.

Levels of Care Covered by Molina Healthcare

Campus Recovery Center offers multiple levels of outpatient care for substance use disorders and co-occurring mental health conditions. Molina Healthcare covers the following programs when deemed medically necessary:

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

PHP provides intensive, structured treatment five to seven days per week for approximately six hours per day. Clients return home or to a sober living environment each evening. Molina covers PHP when clinical criteria demonstrate the need for a high level of structured outpatient care, such as following residential treatment or during periods of acute instability.

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

IOP provides three to five days of treatment per week for approximately three hours per session. This level of care supports clients who need structured treatment while maintaining work, school, or family responsibilities. Molina plans cover IOP as a step-down from PHP or as an appropriate entry point based on clinical assessment.

Outpatient Program (OP)

Standard outpatient treatment includes individual therapy, group counseling, and psychiatric appointments on a weekly or biweekly basis. Molina covers outpatient behavioral health visits as part of its Medicaid and marketplace benefits. This level of care supports long-term recovery and relapse prevention.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

MAT combines FDA-approved medications—such as buprenorphine (Suboxone), naltrexone (Vivitrol), or acamprosate—with counseling and behavioral therapies. Molina Healthcare covers MAT medications and associated clinical services under both Medicaid and marketplace plans. Florida Medicaid through Molina provides comprehensive MAT coverage with minimal cost-sharing.

Behavioral Health Protections Under Federal Law

Molina Healthcare members benefit from several federal laws that protect access to behavioral health treatment:

  • Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA): Requires Molina to provide behavioral health benefits at levels comparable to medical and surgical coverage, including cost-sharing, treatment limits, and prior authorization standards.
  • Affordable Care Act (ACA): Requires marketplace plans to cover substance use disorder and mental health treatment as essential health benefits. Prohibits denial of coverage based on pre-existing conditions.
  • Medicaid Coverage Requirements: Federal Medicaid law requires states to cover certain behavioral health services for eligible populations, and Florida's Medicaid program includes comprehensive substance use disorder treatment benefits.

What to Expect with Molina Healthcare Coverage

Molina Healthcare uses a managed care model for both its Medicaid and marketplace products. This means your plan may require referrals or prior authorization before starting certain levels of care. Campus Recovery Center manages the authorization process on your behalf, working directly with Molina's care management team.

Key details for Molina members:

  • Medicaid Members: Florida Medicaid through Molina typically has no or very low copays for behavioral health services. Some services may require a referral from your primary care provider or prior authorization from Molina.
  • Marketplace Members: Cost-sharing depends on your plan tier (Bronze, Silver, or Gold). You may need to meet your deductible before Molina covers treatment costs. Copays and coinsurance apply to behavioral health visits.
  • Network Status: Verify that Campus Recovery Center is in-network with your specific Molina plan for the lowest out-of-pocket costs.
  • Out-of-Pocket Maximum: All Molina plans have an annual out-of-pocket maximum. Once you reach this limit, Molina covers 100% of covered services for the remainder of the plan year.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Molina Healthcare cover addiction treatment?

Yes. Molina Healthcare covers substance use disorder and mental health treatment through both its Medicaid managed care plans and ACA marketplace plans. Coverage includes PHP, IOP, outpatient counseling, psychiatric services, and Medication-Assisted Treatment. Specific benefits depend on your plan type.

Does Molina Medicaid cover rehab in Florida?

Yes. Molina participates in Florida's Statewide Medicaid Managed Care program and covers behavioral health services including substance use disorder treatment. Florida Medicaid members enrolled in Molina can access PHP, IOP, outpatient therapy, psychiatric services, and MAT with minimal or no cost-sharing.

How do I verify my Molina Healthcare benefits for treatment?

Call Campus Recovery Center at (877) 995-0102. Our admissions team contacts Molina directly to verify your behavioral health benefits, confirm your plan type (Medicaid or Marketplace), and explain any copays, referral requirements, or prior authorization needs. Verification is free and confidential.

What is the difference between Molina Medicaid and Molina Marketplace plans?

Molina Medicaid plans are funded by the state and federal government for individuals who meet income eligibility requirements. These plans typically have no or very low copays. Molina Marketplace plans are purchased through healthcare.gov and have monthly premiums, deductibles, and copays that vary by plan tier. Both plan types cover substance use disorder and mental health treatment.
