Your Rights Under Federal Law

Under the Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA) and the Affordable Care Act (ACA), most insurance plans are required to cover substance use disorder and mental health treatment at levels comparable to medical and surgical care. This means your insurance likely covers:

  • Partial Hospitalization Programs (PHP)
  • Intensive Outpatient Programs (IOP)
  • Outpatient treatment and counseling
  • Psychiatric evaluation and medication management
  • Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

No Insurance? We Can Still Help

Campus Recovery Center offers self-pay options and financial assistance for qualifying individuals. Don't let the cost of treatment prevent you from getting the help you need. Contact our admissions team to discuss payment arrangements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What insurance does Campus Recovery Center accept?

We accept most major insurance plans including Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, United Healthcare, Humana, TRICARE, Medicaid, Medicare, Ambetter, and Molina Healthcare. Contact us to verify your specific plan.

How do I verify my insurance coverage for rehab?

Call our admissions team at (877) 995-0102. We handle the entire verification process and explain your benefits clearly before you begin treatment.

Does insurance cover addiction treatment in Florida?

Yes. Under federal parity laws, most insurance plans must cover substance use disorder and mental health treatment at levels comparable to medical care. Specific coverage varies by plan.

What if I don't have insurance?

We offer self-pay options and financial assistance. Contact our admissions team to discuss what's available for your situation.
