Campus Recovery Center accepts Cigna insurance for addiction and mental health treatment at our Lake Worth Beach, Florida facility. As one of the largest health insurance providers in the United States, Cigna—along with its behavioral health division Evernorth—provides coverage for substance use disorder and mental health services across multiple levels of outpatient care.

Cigna Behavioral Health Coverage

Cigna plans include behavioral health benefits managed through Evernorth (formerly Cigna Behavioral Health). Under the Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA) and the Affordable Care Act (ACA), Cigna is required to cover substance use disorder and mental health treatment at levels comparable to medical and surgical care. This means your Cigna plan likely covers treatment at Campus Recovery Center.

Cigna Plans Accepted

Campus Recovery Center works with most Cigna plan types, including:

  • Cigna PPO – Preferred Provider Organization plans with in-network and out-of-network behavioral health benefits
  • Cigna HMO – Health Maintenance Organization plans requiring referrals for behavioral health services
  • Cigna Open Access Plus (OAP) – Flexible plans allowing access to both in-network and out-of-network providers
  • Cigna EPO – Exclusive Provider Organization plans with in-network behavioral health coverage
  • Cigna Evernorth Behavioral Health Plans – Plans with dedicated behavioral health networks and benefits
  • Cigna Employer-Sponsored Plans – Group health plans offered through employers with behavioral health coverage

Coverage details, copays, deductibles, and out-of-pocket maximums vary by plan. Our admissions team verifies your specific Cigna policy before treatment begins.

Treatment Programs Covered by Cigna

Cigna behavioral health benefits typically cover the following levels of care at Campus Recovery Center:

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

Structured programming five to seven days per week for individuals who need intensive clinical support. PHP includes individual therapy, group counseling, psychiatric evaluation, and medication management. Cigna typically covers PHP when medical necessity criteria are met.

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

Flexible programming three to five days per week, allowing clients to maintain work, school, or family responsibilities while receiving treatment. IOP includes evidence-based therapies, relapse prevention, and clinical support. Cigna plans commonly cover IOP for both substance use and mental health conditions.

Outpatient Treatment (OP)

Individual counseling, group therapy, and psychiatric services on a less intensive schedule. Outpatient treatment serves as a step-down from IOP or as standalone care. Most Cigna plans cover outpatient behavioral health visits.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

FDA-approved medications combined with counseling and behavioral therapies for opioid and alcohol use disorders. Cigna covers MAT medications including buprenorphine, naltrexone, and other prescribed treatments when part of a comprehensive treatment plan.

Dual Diagnosis Treatment

Integrated care for individuals with both a substance use disorder and a co-occurring mental health condition such as depression, anxiety, PTSD, or bipolar disorder. Cigna behavioral health benefits cover dual diagnosis treatment when clinical criteria are satisfied.

How to Verify Your Cigna Insurance

  1. Call Our Admissions Team

    Contact Campus Recovery Center at (877) 995-0102. Have your Cigna insurance card ready, including your member ID and group number. Our admissions team is available to help.

  2. We Contact Cigna Directly

    Our insurance verification specialists contact Cigna to confirm your behavioral health benefits. We determine your coverage for PHP, IOP, outpatient services, and MAT, and identify any prior authorization requirements.

  3. Review Your Benefits

    We explain your Cigna coverage in plain language—what treatment is covered, your copay and deductible amounts, your out-of-pocket maximum, and any session or day limits that apply to your plan.

  4. Begin Treatment

    Once your Cigna benefits are verified, our team coordinates the clinical intake process so you can begin treatment as quickly as possible.

Cigna Behavioral Health Network

Cigna manages its behavioral health benefits through Evernorth, which oversees the network of mental health and substance use disorder treatment providers. Campus Recovery Center works with Cigna and Evernorth to help our clients access their behavioral health benefits. Whether you have in-network or out-of-network coverage, our admissions team can clarify what your Cigna plan covers at our facility.

Many Cigna plans do not require a referral for outpatient behavioral health services, though some HMO and EPO plans may have referral or prior authorization requirements. Our team handles the authorization process on your behalf.

What Information Do I Need to Verify Cigna Coverage?

When you call to verify your Cigna insurance, have the following information available:

  • Cigna member ID number (found on your insurance card)
  • Group number (if applicable)
  • Policyholder name and date of birth
  • Patient name and date of birth (if different from the policyholder)
  • Cigna customer service phone number (on the back of your card)
