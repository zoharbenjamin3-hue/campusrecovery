Medicaid Coverage for Addiction & Mental Health Treatment

Campus Recovery Center accepts Medicaid for addiction and mental health treatment at our Lake Worth Beach, Florida facility. We work with Florida Medicaid managed care plans to provide accessible behavioral health services.

Campus Recovery Center accepts Medicaid for addiction and mental health treatment at our Lake Worth Beach, Florida facility. Medicaid is a joint federal and state program that provides health coverage to eligible low-income individuals and families. In Florida, Medicaid is administered through the Statewide Medicaid Managed Care (SMMC) program, which delivers behavioral health services through managed care organizations.

Florida Medicaid Behavioral Health Coverage

Florida Medicaid provides behavioral health benefits that cover substance use disorder treatment and mental health services for eligible beneficiaries. Under Florida’s managed care system, behavioral health services—including addiction treatment, psychiatric care, and counseling—are covered through managed care plans contracted with the state. Medicaid beneficiaries in Florida are enrolled in a managed care plan that coordinates their behavioral health care.

Medicaid covers behavioral health treatment as a mandatory benefit category. This means eligible Medicaid beneficiaries have access to substance use disorder treatment, mental health counseling, psychiatric services, and medication-assisted treatment through their managed care plan.

Florida Medicaid Managed Care Plans

Campus Recovery Center works with Florida Medicaid managed care plans, including:

  • Sunshine Health – One of Florida’s largest Medicaid managed care plans, providing behavioral health coverage across the state
  • Molina Healthcare of Florida – Medicaid managed care plan offering behavioral health services to eligible members
  • Humana Medical Plan – Medicaid managed care option with integrated behavioral health benefits
  • Simply Healthcare Plans – Medicaid managed care plan with behavioral health coverage in South Florida
  • Aetna Better Health of Florida – Medicaid managed care plan with behavioral health services for eligible members

Coverage and provider network participation vary by managed care plan. Our admissions team verifies your specific Medicaid managed care plan and confirms behavioral health coverage before treatment begins.

Treatment Programs Covered by Medicaid

Florida Medicaid behavioral health benefits typically cover the following levels of care at Campus Recovery Center:

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

Structured programming five to seven days per week for individuals who need intensive clinical support. PHP includes individual therapy, group counseling, psychiatric evaluation, and medication management. Medicaid covers PHP when medical necessity criteria are met and prior authorization is obtained through your managed care plan.

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

Flexible programming three to five days per week, allowing clients to maintain work, school, or family responsibilities while receiving treatment. IOP includes evidence-based therapies, relapse prevention, and clinical support. Florida Medicaid managed care plans cover IOP for both substance use and mental health conditions.

Outpatient Treatment (OP)

Individual counseling, group therapy, and psychiatric services on a less intensive schedule. Outpatient treatment serves as a step-down from IOP or as standalone care. Medicaid covers outpatient behavioral health visits for eligible beneficiaries.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

FDA-approved medications combined with counseling and behavioral therapies for opioid and alcohol use disorders. Florida Medicaid covers MAT medications including buprenorphine, naltrexone, and methadone when part of a comprehensive treatment plan.

Dual Diagnosis Treatment

Integrated care for individuals with both a substance use disorder and a co-occurring mental health condition such as depression, anxiety, PTSD, or bipolar disorder. Medicaid behavioral health benefits cover dual diagnosis treatment when clinical criteria are satisfied.

How to Verify Your Medicaid Benefits

  1. Call Our Admissions Team

    Contact Campus Recovery Center at (877) 995-0102. Have your Medicaid ID card ready, including your Medicaid ID number and managed care plan name. Our admissions team is available to help.

  2. We Verify Your Medicaid Benefits

    Our insurance verification specialists contact your Medicaid managed care plan to confirm your behavioral health benefits. We determine your coverage for PHP, IOP, outpatient services, and MAT, and identify any prior authorization requirements.

  3. Review Your Benefits

    We explain your Medicaid coverage in plain language—what treatment is covered, any copay requirements, and any session or day limits that apply to your managed care plan. Most Medicaid beneficiaries have little to no out-of-pocket costs for behavioral health treatment.

  4. Begin Treatment

    Once your Medicaid benefits are verified and any required authorizations are in place, our team coordinates the clinical intake process so you can begin treatment as quickly as possible.

Medicaid Eligibility in Florida

Florida Medicaid eligibility is based on income, household size, and category of coverage. Eligible groups include low-income families with children, pregnant women, individuals with disabilities, and elderly individuals. Florida has not expanded Medicaid under the Affordable Care Act, so eligibility is limited to specific categories. If you are unsure about your Medicaid eligibility or enrollment status, our admissions team can help you explore your options.

Medicaid managed care plans typically require that members use in-network providers for behavioral health services. Prior authorization may be required for certain levels of care such as partial hospitalization. Our team handles the authorization process on your behalf.

What Information Do I Need to Verify Medicaid Coverage?

When you call to verify your Medicaid benefits, have the following information available:

  • Florida Medicaid ID number (found on your Medicaid card)
  • Managed care plan name (Sunshine Health, Molina, Humana, Simply, Aetna Better Health, etc.)
  • Beneficiary name and date of birth
  • Primary care provider name (if applicable)
  • Managed care plan customer service phone number (on the back of your card)

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Medicaid cover addiction treatment?

Yes. Florida Medicaid covers substance use disorder treatment including partial hospitalization, intensive outpatient, outpatient counseling, and medication-assisted treatment. Medicaid behavioral health benefits are administered through Florida’s Statewide Medicaid Managed Care program at Campus Recovery Center.

Does Florida Medicaid cover mental health treatment at Campus Recovery Center?

Yes. Campus Recovery Center accepts Florida Medicaid for mental health treatment including dual diagnosis care, psychiatric evaluation, medication management, and individual and group therapy across all outpatient levels of care.

How do I verify my Medicaid benefits for rehab?

Call Campus Recovery Center at (877) 995-0102. Our admissions team verifies your Medicaid behavioral health benefits, confirms your managed care plan, and explains what treatment programs are covered under your specific Medicaid plan.

What Florida Medicaid managed care plans does Campus Recovery Center accept?

We work with Florida Medicaid managed care plans including Sunshine Health, Molina Healthcare, Humana, Simply Healthcare, and Aetna Better Health of Florida. Coverage varies by plan, so contact our admissions team to verify your specific Medicaid managed care plan.
