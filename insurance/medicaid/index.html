Medicaid Coverage for Addiction Treatment in Florida

Author: Daniel Touloumis
Editor: Devin Scheller, Director of Operations
Medical Reviewer: Sahil Talwar, PA
Published: August 1, 2024
Last Edited: March 29, 2026

If you or a loved one has Florida Medicaid and needs addiction or mental health treatment, Campus Recovery Center in Lake Worth Beach is here to help. We accept Medicaid for outpatient behavioral health services and work directly with Florida’s Medicaid managed care plans to make treatment accessible and affordable.

How Florida Medicaid Covers Behavioral Health Treatment

Florida Medicaid provides coverage for substance use disorder and mental health treatment under its behavioral health benefit. The program is administered through Statewide Medicaid Managed Care (SMMC) plans, meaning most Medicaid beneficiaries are enrolled in a managed care organization that coordinates their treatment services.

Under both federal and state law, Medicaid is required to cover medically necessary behavioral health services. The Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA) ensures that coverage for substance use disorders and mental health conditions cannot be more restrictive than coverage for medical and surgical conditions.

Who Is Eligible for Florida Medicaid?

Florida Medicaid eligibility is based on income, household size, and category. The following groups may qualify:

  • Low-income parents and caretakers with dependent children
  • Pregnant women with household income at or below 191% of the federal poverty level
  • Children and adolescents under 21 in families meeting income thresholds
  • Individuals with disabilities receiving Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
  • Seniors age 65 and older who meet income and asset requirements
  • Adults without dependents who may qualify through Medicaid expansion or Medically Needy pathways

Not sure if you qualify? Our admissions team can help you understand your eligibility and explore your options. Call (877) 995-0102 for a free, confidential conversation.

Treatment Services Covered by Florida Medicaid

Campus Recovery Center provides the following Medicaid-covered treatment services for substance use disorders and co-occurring mental health conditions:

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

Structured day treatment providing 20 or more hours of clinical services per week. PHP includes individual therapy, group counseling, psychiatric evaluation, and skill-building sessions. Clients return home or to supportive housing in the evenings.

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

A step down from PHP, IOP provides 9 to 19 hours of clinical programming per week. Services include group therapy, individual counseling, relapse prevention planning, and family sessions. IOP allows clients to maintain work or school commitments.

Outpatient Treatment (OP)

Standard outpatient services include individual therapy, psychiatric follow-ups, medication management, and ongoing counseling. Outpatient treatment supports clients in early recovery and those transitioning from higher levels of care.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

Florida Medicaid covers MAT for opioid and alcohol use disorders. MAT combines FDA-approved medications such as buprenorphine (Suboxone), naltrexone (Vivitrol), and methadone with counseling and behavioral therapy for a whole-person approach to recovery.

Additional Medicaid-Covered Behavioral Health Services

  • Comprehensive behavioral health assessments and diagnostic evaluations
  • Individual psychotherapy and cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT)
  • Group therapy and psychoeducation sessions
  • Psychiatric evaluation and medication management
  • Crisis intervention and stabilization services
  • Family therapy and support services
  • Case management and care coordination
  • Peer support specialist services
  • Discharge planning and community resource referrals

Florida Medicaid Managed Care Plans We Work With

Most Florida Medicaid beneficiaries receive their behavioral health benefits through a managed care plan. Campus Recovery Center works with the following Medicaid managed care organizations:

Sunshine Health

One of the largest Medicaid managed care plans in Florida, Sunshine Health provides behavioral health coverage for substance use disorders and mental health treatment.

Molina Healthcare

Molina Healthcare of Florida offers Medicaid managed care with comprehensive behavioral health benefits including outpatient and intensive outpatient treatment.

Humana

Humana’s Florida Medicaid plan covers addiction and mental health treatment services at licensed outpatient behavioral health facilities.

Simply Healthcare

Simply Healthcare provides Medicaid managed care coverage for a full range of behavioral health services including individual, group, and family therapy.

Additional managed care plans we may work with include Prestige Health Choice, Staywell, and Community Care Plan. Contact us to verify your specific plan.

How to Use Your Medicaid Benefits at Campus Recovery Center

  1. Call Our Admissions Team

    Contact us at (877) 995-0102. Have your Medicaid ID card ready. Our team is available to assist you with the verification process.

  2. We Verify Your Medicaid Benefits

    We contact your managed care plan directly to confirm your behavioral health coverage, check for prior authorization requirements, and determine what services are available under your plan.

  3. Prior Authorization (If Required)

    Many Medicaid managed care plans require prior authorization before starting certain levels of care. Our team handles the authorization process on your behalf, working directly with your plan to secure approval.

  4. Begin Treatment

    Once your coverage is confirmed and any required authorizations are in place, you can begin treatment. Florida Medicaid typically covers behavioral health services with no copay or very minimal out-of-pocket costs.

Understanding Prior Authorization for Medicaid

Prior authorization is a process where your Medicaid managed care plan must approve certain treatment services before they begin. This is common for higher levels of care like PHP and IOP. Our clinical and administrative staff are experienced in working with Medicaid managed care organizations and handle prior authorization requests daily. We advocate for the level of care that is clinically appropriate for your needs.

Medicaid and Co-Occurring Disorders

Many individuals seeking addiction treatment also experience co-occurring mental health conditions such as depression, anxiety, PTSD, or bipolar disorder. Florida Medicaid covers integrated treatment for co-occurring disorders, which means you can receive coordinated care for both substance use and mental health at the same time, in the same program.

Campus Recovery Center specializes in dual diagnosis treatment, providing comprehensive clinical services that address the full scope of each client’s behavioral health needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Florida Medicaid cover addiction treatment?

Yes. Florida Medicaid covers substance use disorder treatment including outpatient counseling, intensive outpatient programs, partial hospitalization programs, medication-assisted treatment, and psychiatric services for qualifying beneficiaries.

Who is eligible for Florida Medicaid?

Eligibility includes low-income adults, pregnant women, children, individuals with disabilities, and seniors who meet income thresholds set by the state. Contact our admissions team at (877) 995-0102 to discuss your eligibility.

Does Medicaid cover Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)?

Yes. Florida Medicaid covers MAT including Suboxone (buprenorphine/naloxone), naltrexone (Vivitrol), and methadone for the treatment of opioid and alcohol use disorders. MAT is combined with counseling and therapy for a comprehensive recovery approach.

Which Medicaid managed care plans does Campus Recovery Center accept?

We work with Florida Medicaid managed care plans including Sunshine Health, Molina Healthcare, Humana, Simply Healthcare, Prestige Health Choice, and Staywell. Call (877) 995-0102 to verify your specific plan.
