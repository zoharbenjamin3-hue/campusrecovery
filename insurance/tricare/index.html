TRICARE Coverage for Addiction Treatment

Author: Daniel Touloumis
Editor: Devin Scheller, Director of Operations
Medical Reviewer: Sahil Talwar, PA
Published: August 1, 2024
Last Edited: March 29, 2026

Campus Recovery Center accepts TRICARE for addiction and mental health treatment at our Lake Worth Beach, Florida facility. We are honored to serve active duty service members, military retirees, National Guard and Reserve members, and their families. Our clinical team understands the unique challenges faced by the military community and provides evidence-based treatment tailored to the needs of those who have served.

TRICARE Behavioral Health Benefits

TRICARE provides comprehensive behavioral health coverage for substance use disorders and mental health conditions. As the health care program for the U.S. military community, TRICARE covers treatment that is medically necessary and provided by TRICARE-authorized providers. Behavioral health benefits include:

  • Substance use disorder assessment, diagnosis, and treatment
  • Individual, group, and family therapy
  • Psychiatric evaluation and medication management
  • Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) for opioid and alcohol use disorders
  • Dual diagnosis treatment for co-occurring mental health conditions
  • Crisis intervention and stabilization
  • Relapse prevention and continuing care planning

TRICARE Plans We Accept

Campus Recovery Center accepts multiple TRICARE plan options. Each plan has different cost-sharing structures, referral requirements, and eligibility criteria.

TRICARE Prime

TRICARE Prime is a managed care option available to active duty service members (who are automatically enrolled), active duty family members, retirees, and retiree family members. Prime requires a primary care manager (PCM) referral for most behavioral health services. Active duty members have no out-of-pocket costs. Other Prime beneficiaries pay annual enrollment fees and fixed copays per visit.

TRICARE Select

TRICARE Select is a self-managed preferred provider option available to non-active-duty beneficiaries. Select does not require referrals for behavioral health treatment at TRICARE-authorized providers. Beneficiaries pay annual deductibles and cost-shares that vary based on sponsor status (active duty family, retiree, or retiree family member).

TRICARE Reserve Select

TRICARE Reserve Select (TRS) is a premium-based plan available to qualified members of the Selected Reserve and their families. TRS provides coverage similar to TRICARE Select, including behavioral health benefits for addiction and mental health treatment, with applicable deductibles and cost-shares.

TRICARE Retired Reserve & Young Adult

TRICARE Retired Reserve covers qualified retired Reserve members and their families. TRICARE Young Adult extends coverage to dependent adult children up to age 26. Both plans include behavioral health benefits covering substance use disorder and mental health treatment services.

Levels of Care Covered by TRICARE

TRICARE covers multiple levels of outpatient behavioral health treatment at Campus Recovery Center. The appropriate level of care is determined by clinical assessment and must meet medical necessity criteria.

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

PHP provides the most intensive outpatient treatment, typically five to seven days per week for approximately six hours per day. TRICARE covers PHP for individuals who require intensive structured treatment but do not need 24-hour inpatient supervision. PHP is commonly used as a step-down from residential care or for those presenting with high clinical acuity. Treatment includes individual therapy, group counseling, psychiatric services, and specialized programming for trauma, PTSD, and military-related stressors.

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

IOP provides structured treatment three to five days per week for approximately three hours per session. TRICARE covers IOP as a step-down from PHP or as a primary treatment option for individuals with moderate treatment needs. Our IOP incorporates evidence-based therapies including cognitive behavioral therapy, dialectical behavior therapy, EMDR for trauma processing, and relapse prevention. Group programming addresses the specific experiences of military service members and their families.

Outpatient Treatment (OP)

Standard outpatient treatment includes individual therapy, psychiatric appointments, and medication management. TRICARE covers outpatient behavioral health visits with applicable cost-shares. Outpatient care provides ongoing support for individuals transitioning from intensive programming or those who benefit from regular therapeutic engagement.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

TRICARE covers Medication-Assisted Treatment for opioid and alcohol use disorders. MAT combines FDA-approved medications—including buprenorphine (Suboxone), naltrexone (Vivitrol), and acamprosate—with behavioral therapy. Campus Recovery Center provides MAT integrated into all levels of care, with psychiatric oversight ensuring safe and effective medication management.

Military-Specific Behavioral Health Challenges

Service members, veterans, and military families face unique stressors that can contribute to substance use and mental health conditions. Campus Recovery Center provides treatment that addresses these military-specific challenges:

  • Combat-related trauma and PTSD: Exposure to combat and operational stress can lead to post-traumatic stress disorder, which frequently co-occurs with substance use disorders. Our clinicians use trauma-informed approaches including EMDR and Cognitive Processing Therapy.
  • Moral injury: The psychological distress from actions or experiences that violate a service member's moral code requires specialized therapeutic approaches integrated into addiction treatment.
  • Military sexual trauma (MST): Survivors of MST often develop co-occurring PTSD and substance use disorders. Our team provides trauma-sensitive care in a safe clinical environment.
  • Transition stress: The challenges of transitioning from military to civilian life—including loss of identity, purpose, and community—contribute to substance use and mental health conditions in the veteran population.
  • Family impact: Deployments, relocations, and the demands of military life affect the entire family system. Our programming addresses family dynamics as part of comprehensive treatment.

TRICARE Referral and Authorization Process

  1. Contact Our Admissions Team

    Call (877) 995-0102 with your TRICARE information, including your plan type and sponsor's Defense Enrollment Eligibility Reporting System (DEERS) status. Our team is experienced with TRICARE processes and will guide you through every step.

  2. Referral Coordination (If Required)

    If you have TRICARE Prime or are an active duty service member, a referral from your primary care manager (PCM) is typically required. Our admissions team can coordinate with your PCM to obtain the referral. TRICARE Select beneficiaries do not need a referral.

  3. Benefits Verification and Authorization

    We verify your TRICARE benefits and submit any required prior authorization requests. We work directly with Health Net Federal Services (TRICARE West) or Humana Military (TRICARE East) depending on your region to secure treatment approval.

  4. Begin Treatment

    Once authorization is confirmed, you begin treatment with full knowledge of your coverage, cost-shares, and treatment plan. Our team coordinates with TRICARE throughout your episode of care.

Understanding Your TRICARE Costs

TRICARE cost-sharing for behavioral health treatment varies by plan type and sponsor status:

  • Active duty service members (TRICARE Prime): No out-of-pocket costs for covered behavioral health services.
  • Active duty family members: No enrollment fee for TRICARE Prime. TRICARE Select requires annual deductibles. Cost-shares are lower than those for retiree categories.
  • Retirees and retiree family members: Annual enrollment fees apply for TRICARE Prime. TRICARE Select requires annual deductibles and percentage-based cost-shares for covered services.
  • TRICARE Reserve Select: Monthly premiums, annual deductibles, and cost-shares apply. Rates are published annually by the Defense Health Agency.
  • Catastrophic cap: TRICARE sets an annual catastrophic cap that limits your total out-of-pocket expenses. Once reached, TRICARE covers all additional costs for the remainder of the fiscal year.

Confidentiality for Active Duty Service Members

We understand that confidentiality is a significant concern for active duty service members seeking addiction or mental health treatment. While TRICARE is a military health benefit, federal law and regulations provide important privacy protections. Campus Recovery Center follows all applicable HIPAA and 42 CFR Part 2 confidentiality requirements for substance use disorder treatment records. Our admissions team can discuss specific confidentiality protections that apply to your situation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does TRICARE cover addiction treatment?

Yes. TRICARE covers substance use disorder treatment including partial hospitalization (PHP), intensive outpatient (IOP), outpatient therapy, and medication-assisted treatment (MAT). Benefits are available to active duty service members, retirees, and their eligible dependents.

Does TRICARE require a referral for behavioral health treatment?

TRICARE Prime requires a referral from your primary care manager (PCM) for most behavioral health services. TRICARE Select does not require referrals at TRICARE-authorized providers. Active duty service members need a referral regardless of plan type. Our admissions team helps coordinate referrals when needed.

What TRICARE plans does Campus Recovery Center accept?

We accept TRICARE Prime, TRICARE Select, TRICARE Reserve Select, TRICARE Retired Reserve, TRICARE Young Adult, and TRICARE For Life. Call (877) 995-0102 to verify your specific TRICARE benefits.

Can veterans use TRICARE for rehab at Campus Recovery Center?

Military retirees and their dependents who maintain TRICARE eligibility can use their benefits at Campus Recovery Center. Veterans who separated from service and transitioned to VA healthcare may have different coverage options. Contact our admissions team to discuss your specific eligibility and coverage.
