Blue Cross Blue Shield Insurance for Addiction Treatment

Author: Daniel Touloumis
Editor: Devin Scheller, Director of Operations
Medical Reviewer: Sahil Talwar, PA
Published: August 1, 2024
Last Edited: March 29, 2026

Campus Recovery Center accepts Blue Cross Blue Shield (BCBS) insurance for addiction and mental health treatment in Lake Worth Beach, Florida. As the largest health insurer in the United States, BCBS operates through a network of independent regional companies. Our admissions team works with all BCBS affiliates to verify your coverage quickly and confidentially.

BCBS Plans We Work With

Blue Cross Blue Shield is a federation of 34 independent, locally operated companies. Each affiliate offers its own plans, but most include behavioral health benefits that cover substance use disorder and mental health treatment. Plans we commonly work with include:

Florida Blue

Florida's BCBS affiliate. Florida Blue plans typically cover PHP, IOP, and outpatient treatment for addiction and mental health conditions at in-network facilities.

Anthem Blue Cross Blue Shield

Anthem operates in 14 states and offers commercial, Medicaid, and Medicare plans with behavioral health benefits for substance use disorder treatment.

Blue Cross Blue Shield of Illinois

BCBS-IL members seeking out-of-state treatment in Florida can access coverage through the BlueCard program, which allows use of providers nationwide.

Other BCBS Affiliates

Through the BlueCard program, BCBS members from any state can access covered services at participating providers across the country, including Campus Recovery Center.

What BCBS Covers for Addiction Treatment

Most Blue Cross Blue Shield plans include behavioral health benefits that cover evidence-based addiction and mental health treatment. Under the Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA), BCBS plans must cover substance use disorder treatment at levels comparable to medical and surgical care. Services typically covered include:

  • Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP) — Structured daytime treatment with medical oversight, therapy sessions, and psychiatric care, typically five to seven days per week.
  • Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP) — Flexible evening or daytime group and individual therapy sessions, typically three to five days per week, allowing clients to maintain daily responsibilities.
  • Outpatient Treatment (OP) — Individual therapy, group counseling, and aftercare services on a weekly or biweekly basis for ongoing support.
  • Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) — FDA-approved medications combined with counseling and behavioral therapies to treat opioid and alcohol use disorders.
  • Psychiatric Evaluation & Medication Management — Initial psychiatric assessment and ongoing medication monitoring for co-occurring mental health conditions.
  • Individual and Group Therapy — Evidence-based approaches including cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), dialectical behavior therapy (DBT), and trauma-focused therapy.

In-Network vs. Out-of-Network BCBS Coverage

Your out-of-pocket costs depend on whether Campus Recovery Center is classified as in-network or out-of-network under your specific BCBS plan. Here is how these designations typically affect your coverage:

  • In-Network Benefits: Lower copays, lower deductibles, and a higher percentage of treatment costs covered by BCBS. Prior authorization may still be required for certain levels of care.
  • Out-of-Network Benefits: Many BCBS plans offer out-of-network coverage at a reduced benefit level. You may pay a higher deductible and coinsurance rate, but coverage is often still available.
  • BlueCard Program: If you hold a BCBS plan from another state, the BlueCard program enables you to access treatment at participating providers in Florida. Our team handles the coordination with your home plan.

Our admissions team will verify your specific network status and explain your financial responsibility before you begin treatment. Call (877) 995-0102 for a confidential benefits check.

Behavioral Health Benefits Under BCBS

BCBS behavioral health benefits are designed to provide coverage for both substance use disorder and mental health treatment. Key aspects of these benefits include:

  • Substance Use Disorder Coverage: Treatment for alcohol use disorder, opioid use disorder, stimulant use disorders, and other drug dependencies at clinically appropriate levels of care.
  • Mental Health Coverage: Treatment for depression, anxiety, PTSD, bipolar disorder, and other mental health conditions that commonly co-occur with addiction.
  • Dual Diagnosis Treatment: Integrated care for individuals managing both substance use disorder and a co-occurring mental health condition, which is critical for lasting recovery.
  • Parity Protections: Federal law requires BCBS to apply the same treatment limits, copay structures, and prior authorization requirements to behavioral health as it does to medical and surgical benefits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Blue Cross Blue Shield cover rehab?

Yes. Most BCBS plans cover substance use disorder and mental health treatment, including partial hospitalization, intensive outpatient, outpatient therapy, and medication-assisted treatment. Coverage specifics depend on your individual plan, network status, and whether prior authorization is required.

Does Florida Blue cover addiction treatment at Campus Recovery Center?

Yes. Florida Blue is the BCBS affiliate for the state of Florida. Campus Recovery Center works with Florida Blue plans to provide coverage for addiction and mental health treatment at our Lake Worth Beach facility. Call (877) 995-0102 to verify your specific Florida Blue benefits.

What is the difference between in-network and out-of-network BCBS coverage for rehab?

In-network coverage typically means lower copays, lower deductibles, and a higher percentage of treatment costs covered by your insurer. Out-of-network coverage may still be available under many BCBS plans but generally involves higher out-of-pocket costs. Our admissions team can verify your network status and explain your financial responsibility before treatment begins.

How do I verify my BCBS benefits for addiction treatment?

Call Campus Recovery Center at (877) 995-0102. Our admissions team will contact BCBS directly, verify your behavioral health benefits, and explain your coverage including copays, deductibles, and out-of-pocket costs before you begin treatment. The verification process is free and confidential.
