Aetna Insurance Coverage for Addiction Treatment

Author: Daniel Touloumis
Editor: Devin Scheller, Director of Operations
Medical Reviewer: Sahil Talwar, PA
Published: August 1, 2024
Last Edited: March 29, 2026

Campus Recovery Center is an in-network Aetna provider for addiction and mental health treatment in Lake Worth Beach, Florida. If you or a loved one carries an Aetna insurance plan, your behavioral health benefits likely cover the full continuum of outpatient care we offer—from partial hospitalization through outpatient counseling and medication-assisted treatment.

Aetna Behavioral Health Coverage

Aetna provides behavioral health benefits through most of its plan types, including employer-sponsored group plans, individual and family marketplace plans, Aetna Medicare Advantage, and Aetna Student Health plans. Under federal law and Aetna's own behavioral health policies, substance use disorder and mental health treatment are classified as essential health benefits.

As an in-network Aetna provider, Campus Recovery Center works directly with Aetna to authorize treatment, submit claims, and reduce your out-of-pocket expenses. Using an in-network facility typically means lower copays, reduced deductibles, and streamlined approvals compared to seeking care out of network.

Treatment Programs Covered by Aetna

Aetna behavioral health benefits generally cover the following levels of care and services provided at Campus Recovery Center:

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

Structured full-day programming five to seven days per week. PHP provides intensive clinical treatment including individual therapy, group counseling, psychiatric services, and evidence-based interventions for substance use disorders and co-occurring mental health conditions.

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

Three to five days per week of structured programming that allows clients to maintain work, school, or family responsibilities. IOP includes group therapy, individual counseling, relapse prevention, and skill-building sessions.

Outpatient Treatment (OP)

Flexible individual therapy and counseling sessions scheduled weekly or biweekly. Outpatient care serves as a step-down from IOP or as standalone treatment for individuals with less intensive clinical needs.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

FDA-approved medications such as Suboxone (buprenorphine/naloxone) and Vivitrol (naltrexone) combined with counseling and behavioral therapies. MAT is covered under Aetna's pharmacy and behavioral health benefits for opioid and alcohol use disorders.

Psychiatric Evaluation & Medication Management

Comprehensive psychiatric assessments, diagnosis, and ongoing medication management for co-occurring mental health disorders including depression, anxiety, PTSD, and bipolar disorder.

Individual & Group Therapy

Evidence-based therapies including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), trauma-focused therapy, motivational interviewing, and psychoeducation groups.

Mental Health Parity and Your Aetna Coverage

The Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act (MHPAEA) requires that health insurance plans, including Aetna plans, provide behavioral health benefits that are no more restrictive than benefits for medical and surgical care. This federal protection applies to financial requirements such as copays and deductibles, as well as treatment limitations including visit limits and prior authorization requirements.

The Affordable Care Act (ACA) further reinforces these protections by classifying mental health and substance use disorder services as essential health benefits. If your Aetna plan is subject to ACA requirements, it must cover behavioral health treatment without annual or lifetime dollar limits.

If you believe your Aetna plan has unfairly denied or limited your behavioral health benefits, our admissions team can assist with the appeals process and help you understand your rights under federal parity law.

What to Expect: Costs with Aetna Insurance

Your out-of-pocket costs for addiction treatment with Aetna depend on several factors specific to your plan:

  • Deductible – The amount you pay before Aetna begins covering treatment costs. Some plans have separate behavioral health deductibles.
  • Copay – A fixed amount you pay per visit or per day of treatment (for example, $30 per outpatient visit).
  • Coinsurance – The percentage of treatment costs you are responsible for after meeting your deductible (for example, Aetna pays 80% and you pay 20%).
  • Out-of-Pocket Maximum – The most you will pay in a plan year. Once you reach this limit, Aetna covers 100% of eligible services.

As an in-network provider, Campus Recovery Center's negotiated rates with Aetna help keep your costs as low as your plan allows. Our admissions team explains all cost details before treatment begins—there are no surprise bills.

Frequently Asked Questions About Aetna Coverage for Rehab

Does Aetna cover rehab and addiction treatment?

Yes. Aetna covers substance use disorder and mental health treatment under most plan types, including employer-sponsored, marketplace, and Medicare Advantage plans. Coverage typically includes partial hospitalization (PHP), intensive outpatient (IOP), outpatient counseling, medication-assisted treatment (MAT), and psychiatric services. Specific benefits vary by plan, so contact us to verify your individual coverage.

How do I verify my Aetna benefits for rehab?

Call Campus Recovery Center at (877) 995-0102. Our admissions team contacts Aetna directly to verify your behavioral health benefits, confirm your copay, deductible, and out-of-pocket costs, and explain your coverage in plain language before treatment begins. The process is free, confidential, and typically completed within minutes.

Does Aetna require preauthorization for addiction treatment?

Many Aetna plans require prior authorization for behavioral health treatment, particularly at the PHP and IOP levels of care. Campus Recovery Center handles the preauthorization process directly with Aetna on your behalf, including submitting clinical documentation and coordinating with Aetna's utilization management team. You do not need to manage this paperwork yourself.

What levels of care does Aetna cover at Campus Recovery Center?

Aetna covers multiple levels of outpatient addiction and mental health treatment at Campus Recovery Center, including Partial Hospitalization Programs (PHP), Intensive Outpatient Programs (IOP), standard outpatient therapy, medication-assisted treatment (MAT) with Suboxone or Vivitrol, and psychiatric evaluation and medication management. The level of care recommended depends on the results of your clinical assessment.
