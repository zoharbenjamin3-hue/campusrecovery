Careers at Campus Recovery Center

Make a meaningful difference in people's lives every day. Join our compassionate, mission-driven team.

Campus Recovery Center is always looking for compassionate, dedicated professionals who are passionate about helping individuals recover from addiction and mental health conditions. Join our team and make a meaningful difference in people's lives every day.

As a Joint Commission accredited outpatient treatment facility in Lake Worth Beach, Florida, we hold ourselves to the highest standards of clinical quality and patient safety. Our staff members are the backbone of that commitment — and we invest in every team member's growth, well-being, and professional satisfaction.

Why Work With Us

  • Mission-driven work — Help clients transform their lives through evidence-based treatment. Every day, you'll see the direct impact of your work on individuals and families affected by addiction.
  • Collaborative environment — Work alongside a team of highly credentialed clinical professionals including licensed therapists, physician assistants, and certified addiction counselors.
  • Joint Commission accredited — Be part of a facility recognized for meeting the most rigorous quality and safety standards in behavioral healthcare.
  • Professional development — We support continuing education, clinical supervision hours, and career advancement. Staff regularly attend conferences, workshops, and specialized training in evidence-based modalities.
  • Competitive compensation — We offer competitive salaries, health benefits, paid time off, and a supportive work-life balance in a field that truly matters.
  • South Florida location — Work in the heart of Lake Worth Beach, minutes from the coast, with easy access to Delray Beach, West Palm Beach, and all of Palm Beach County.

Our Culture

At Campus Recovery Center, we believe that exceptional client outcomes start with a supported, empowered team. Our culture is built on mutual respect, clinical integrity, and a genuine passion for recovery. We encourage open communication, multidisciplinary collaboration, and innovation in treatment approaches.

Our team includes professionals from diverse backgrounds — psychology, social work, nursing, addiction counseling, and more — united by a shared belief that recovery is possible for everyone. Whether you're early in your career or a seasoned clinician, you'll find mentorship and growth opportunities here.

Areas We Hire For

Clinical Staff

Licensed therapists (LMHC, LCSW, LMFT), mental health counselors, certified addiction professionals (CAP, MCAP), and registered interns with experience in substance use disorders, mental health treatment, and co-occurring conditions.

Typical roles: Primary Therapist, Group Facilitator, Family Therapist, Clinical Supervisor

Medical Staff

Physicians (MD/DO), physician assistants (PA), psychiatric nurse practitioners (APRN), and registered nurses (RN) with experience in behavioral health, addiction medicine, MAT protocols, and psychiatric evaluation.

Typical roles: Medical Director, Prescribing Provider, Nursing Staff, Medical Reviewer

Support & Administrative Staff

Case managers, behavioral health technicians (BHTs), outreach coordinators, alumni coordinators, intake specialists, and administrative professionals who share our commitment to client care.

Typical roles: Case Manager, BHT, Admissions Coordinator, Office Administrator, Insurance Verification Specialist

What We Look For

Beyond credentials and experience, we value team members who demonstrate:

  • Empathy and compassion — A genuine desire to support individuals through one of the most challenging periods of their lives.
  • Clinical curiosity — An interest in staying current with best practices in addiction medicine and mental health treatment.
  • Accountability — Commitment to ethical practice, accurate documentation, and transparent communication with clients and colleagues.
  • Resilience — The ability to maintain professional boundaries and self-care while working in a demanding clinical environment.
  • Team orientation — Willingness to collaborate across disciplines — clinical, medical, and administrative — to deliver the best outcomes for every client.

Benefits

  • Competitive salary commensurate with experience and licensure
  • Health, dental, and vision insurance
  • Paid time off and holidays
  • Continuing education stipend and paid training days
  • Clinical supervision hours for registered interns
  • Supportive, team-oriented work environment
  • Opportunity to work in a growing, mission-driven organization

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you offer clinical supervision hours?

Yes. We provide supervision for registered interns (RMHCI, RCSWI) working toward full licensure in the state of Florida. Supervision is provided by licensed clinical supervisors on staff.

What populations do you serve?

We treat adults (18+) with substance use disorders, co-occurring mental health conditions, and dual diagnosis. Our programs include Partial Hospitalization (PHP), Intensive Outpatient (IOP), and standard Outpatient (OP) levels of care.

Is prior experience in addiction treatment required?

For clinical roles, experience or strong interest in addiction treatment is preferred. For support and administrative roles, a commitment to our mission and willingness to learn are most important. We provide onboarding and ongoing training for all new team members.
