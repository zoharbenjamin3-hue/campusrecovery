JCAHO Accreditation

Author: Daniel Touloumis
Editor: Devin Scheller, Director of Operations
Medical Reviewer: Sahil Talwar, PA
Published: August 1, 2024
Last Edited: March 29, 2026

Campus Recovery Center is proud to be accredited by The Joint Commission (formerly known as JCAHO — the Joint Commission on Accreditation of Healthcare Organizations). This accreditation represents our commitment to the highest standards of quality care, patient safety, and continuous performance improvement.

What Is The Joint Commission?

The Joint Commission is an independent, nonprofit organization that accredits and certifies healthcare organizations across the United States. Founded in 1951, it is recognized nationwide as a symbol of quality in healthcare, evaluating facilities on rigorous performance standards encompassing patient care, safety, infection control, medication management, and organizational leadership.

The Gold Seal of Approval

Earning The Joint Commission's Gold Seal of Approval signifies that Campus Recovery Center has demonstrated compliance with the organization's comprehensive performance standards. This seal is widely recognized by insurers, healthcare providers, and patients as a mark of excellence in healthcare delivery.

What Accreditation Means for You

  • Quality assurance — Our facility meets or exceeds nationally recognized standards for patient care and safety.
  • Continuous improvement — We undergo regular on-site surveys (at least every three years) to maintain accreditation and drive ongoing improvements.
  • Patient safety — Rigorous protocols are in place for medication management, infection prevention, emergency preparedness, and risk reduction.
  • Accountability — Accreditation holds our facility to a higher standard of transparency and organizational governance.
  • Evidence-based practices — Our clinical programs are built on evidence-based treatment approaches verified through the accreditation process.

The Accreditation Process

Joint Commission accreditation involves a comprehensive evaluation process:

  1. Application and self-assessment — The facility applies and conducts an internal review of its practices and policies.
  2. On-site survey — Joint Commission surveyors conduct a thorough, multi-day evaluation of all aspects of patient care, safety, and organizational management.
  3. Standards review — The facility is evaluated against hundreds of performance standards covering everything from clinical care to emergency management.
  4. Accreditation decision — Based on survey findings, The Joint Commission issues an accreditation decision.
  5. Ongoing monitoring — Accredited facilities must maintain standards continuously and undergo re-survey at regular intervals.

Our Commitment

At Campus Recovery Center, accreditation is not a one-time achievement—it is an ongoing commitment to excellence. Our leadership, clinical team, and support staff work daily to uphold the standards that earned us The Joint Commission's Gold Seal of Approval, ensuring every client receives the highest quality care possible.
