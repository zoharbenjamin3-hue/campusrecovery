Campus Recovery Center is proud to be accredited by The Joint Commission (formerly known as JCAHO — the Joint Commission on Accreditation of Healthcare Organizations). This accreditation represents our commitment to the highest standards of quality care, patient safety, and continuous performance improvement.

What Is The Joint Commission?

The Joint Commission is an independent, nonprofit organization that accredits and certifies healthcare organizations across the United States. Founded in 1951, it is recognized nationwide as a symbol of quality in healthcare, evaluating facilities on rigorous performance standards encompassing patient care, safety, infection control, medication management, and organizational leadership.

The Gold Seal of Approval

Earning The Joint Commission's Gold Seal of Approval signifies that Campus Recovery Center has demonstrated compliance with the organization's comprehensive performance standards. This seal is widely recognized by insurers, healthcare providers, and patients as a mark of excellence in healthcare delivery.

What Accreditation Means for You

  • Quality assurance — Our facility meets or exceeds nationally recognized standards for patient care and safety.
  • Continuous improvement — We undergo regular on-site surveys (at least every three years) to maintain accreditation and drive ongoing improvements.
  • Patient safety — Rigorous protocols are in place for medication management, infection prevention, emergency preparedness, and risk reduction.
  • Accountability — Accreditation holds our facility to a higher standard of transparency and organizational governance.
  • Evidence-based practices — Our clinical programs are built on evidence-based treatment approaches verified through the accreditation process.

The Accreditation Process

Joint Commission accreditation involves a comprehensive evaluation process:

  1. Application and self-assessment — The facility applies and conducts an internal review of its practices and policies.
  2. On-site survey — Joint Commission surveyors conduct a thorough, multi-day evaluation of all aspects of patient care, safety, and organizational management.
  3. Standards review — The facility is evaluated against hundreds of performance standards covering everything from clinical care to emergency management.
  4. Accreditation decision — Based on survey findings, The Joint Commission issues an accreditation decision.
  5. Ongoing monitoring — Accredited facilities must maintain standards continuously and undergo re-survey at regular intervals.

Why Accreditation Matters When Choosing a Rehab

Not all treatment centers are created equal. When choosing a rehab facility, Joint Commission accreditation is one of the most reliable indicators of quality. Here's why it should factor into your decision:

  • Insurance acceptance — Many insurance companies require or prefer Joint Commission accredited facilities. Accreditation can streamline insurance verification and improve coverage approval rates.
  • Regulatory compliance — Accredited facilities meet or exceed state and federal healthcare regulations, giving families peace of mind that their loved one is in a safe, compliant environment.
  • Staff qualifications — The Joint Commission evaluates staff credentials, training, and competency. At Campus Recovery Center, our therapists are licensed, master's-level educated, or Certified Addiction Professional (CAP) credentialed.
  • Treatment effectiveness — Accredited facilities must demonstrate that their treatment approaches are evidence-based and produce measurable outcomes.
  • Patient rights — The Joint Commission enforces strict standards around informed consent, confidentiality, patient grievance processes, and ethical treatment practices.

Standards We Meet

The Joint Commission evaluates behavioral healthcare organizations across hundreds of specific performance standards. Key areas where Campus Recovery Center demonstrates compliance include:

  • Client assessment and care — Comprehensive clinical assessments at intake, individualized treatment planning, and regular progress reviews.
  • Medication management — Safe prescribing, monitoring, and administration protocols for all medications including MAT (Medication-Assisted Treatment).
  • Infection prevention and control — Policies and procedures to maintain a safe, hygienic clinical environment.
  • Emergency management — Preparedness plans for medical emergencies, psychiatric crises, and natural disasters.
  • Human resources — Verification of staff credentials, background checks, ongoing competency evaluations, and clinical supervision.
  • Performance improvement — Data collection and analysis to continuously measure and improve treatment outcomes, client satisfaction, and operational efficiency.
  • Rights and responsibilities — Policies ensuring clients are treated with dignity, informed of their rights, and involved in their own treatment decisions.
  • Leadership and governance — Organizational structure that supports safe, ethical, and effective care delivery.

Frequently Asked Questions About Accreditation

Is Joint Commission accreditation required for rehab facilities?

No. Accreditation is voluntary. Many treatment centers operate without it. The fact that Campus Recovery Center has pursued and earned accreditation demonstrates our commitment to exceeding minimum regulatory requirements.

How often is accreditation renewed?

Joint Commission accreditation is valid for up to three years. Facilities undergo comprehensive on-site surveys to maintain their status. Between surveys, accredited organizations must continuously meet all standards.

Does accreditation affect my insurance coverage?

In many cases, yes. Several major insurers — including Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, United Healthcare, and others — give preference to Joint Commission accredited facilities. This can simplify the insurance verification process and improve the likelihood of coverage approval.

How can I verify that Campus Recovery Center is accredited?

You can verify our accreditation status directly through The Joint Commission's Quality Check website, or by contacting our admissions team at (877) 995-0102.
