Clinical Addiction Services

Author: Daniel Touloumis
Editor: Devin Scheller, Director of Operations
Medical Reviewer: Sahil Talwar, PA
Published: August 1, 2024
Last Edited: March 29, 2026

Campus Recovery Center delivers a comprehensive continuum of clinical addiction services. Every level of care includes individual counseling, group counseling, medical and psychiatric care, case management, and ancillary services—all tailored to each client's individual needs.

Levels of Care

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

Our most intensive outpatient option, designed to help individuals reintegrate into daily living after stabilization at detox or a residential program.

  • Schedule: 7 days/week, 5 hours/day (Monday–Sunday)
  • Full daily schedule of individual and group sessions
  • Medical assessments, psychiatric evaluations, and medication management
  • Dedicated case manager for practical support needs

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

Provides the structure and education necessary for individuals to succeed in an increasingly independent environment.

  • Schedule: Begins at 6 days/week, 3 hours/day
  • Clinical hours reduce as the individual progresses (3–5 days/week)
  • Schedules individualized from Monday–Sunday programming
  • All core services included: therapy, psychiatric care, case management

Outpatient Program (OP)

Continued professional care as individuals progress to a self-directed program.

  • Schedule: 2 days/week
  • One day of individual counseling (1 hour)
  • One day of group counseling
  • Ongoing medical, psychiatric, case management, and ancillary services

Core Services

Individual Counseling

One-hour confidential, private appointments with a primary therapist assigned for the full duration of the program. Primary therapists are licensed, master's-level educated, or Certified Addiction Professional (CAP) credentialed. The therapeutic relationship is foundational to the recovery process.

Group Counseling

Facilitated sessions among 12–15 clients, covering critical topics for sustained recovery:

  • Relapse prevention and stress management
  • Psychoeducational content and life skills development
  • Belief systems and process groups
  • Trauma, depression, anxiety, substance use, and grief

Medical & Psychiatric Care

Every client receives comprehensive health assessments and physicals, including evaluation for disease, harm risk, and nutritional needs. A psychiatric nurse practitioner evaluates individuals for mental health conditions and works collaboratively to create individualized medication protocols.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

Anti-craving medications may be recommended based on medical necessity to prevent relapses for opioid or alcohol use disorders. Available medications include:

  • Vivitrol (injectable Naltrexone) — monthly injection
  • Oral Naltrexone — daily oral medication
  • Suboxone — for opioid use disorder management

All medications are monitored and delivered monthly by qualified medical professionals.

Case Management

A dedicated case manager assists each client with practical needs including food stamps, ID cards, court and legal documentation, resume writing, and other essential supports for rebuilding a stable life in recovery.

Ancillary Services

12-Step programming, connections to local community resources for long-term sobriety maintenance, and a comprehensive aftercare support network upon program graduation.

Verify Your Insurance

Most insurance plans accepted. Our admissions team will verify your coverage and explain your options.
