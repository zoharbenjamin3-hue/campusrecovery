Clinical Addiction Services

A comprehensive continuum of clinical addiction services tailored to each client's individual needs—from intensive day programs to flexible outpatient care.

This page provides a detailed look at every clinical service included in your treatment at Campus Recovery Center. Whether you're in our PHP, IOP, or Outpatient program, these services form the foundation of your individualized treatment plan. For an overview of program levels and daily structure, visit our Clinical Programs page.

Individual Therapy

Individual therapy is the cornerstone of treatment at Campus Recovery Center. Every client is assigned a primary therapist who remains with them for the full duration of their program — building consistency, trust, and therapeutic depth that short-term or rotating assignments cannot match.

What Happens in a Session

Sessions are one hour, conducted in a private, confidential setting. Your therapist will work with you to:

  • Identify and challenge thought patterns that drive substance use or mental health symptoms
  • Process trauma, grief, and unresolved emotional experiences
  • Build coping strategies for triggers, cravings, and high-risk situations
  • Set and track measurable recovery goals
  • Develop relapse prevention and crisis management plans

Therapeutic Modalities

Our therapists are trained in multiple evidence-based approaches and select the modality best suited to each client:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) — Restructuring negative thought patterns that contribute to addiction and mental health symptoms.
  • Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) — Building skills in distress tolerance, emotional regulation, mindfulness, and interpersonal effectiveness.
  • Motivational Interviewing (MI) — Strengthening internal motivation for change through collaborative, non-confrontational conversation.
  • Trauma-Focused Therapies — Including EMDR and trauma-informed CBT for clients with PTSD or complex trauma histories.
  • Solution-Focused Brief Therapy — Goal-oriented work focused on building solutions rather than analyzing problems.

Who Provides It

All primary therapists are licensed, master's-level clinicians (LMHC, LCSW, LMFT) or Certified Addiction Professionals (CAP/MCAP). Many hold dual credentials in both mental health and addiction counseling.

Group Therapy

Group therapy provides something individual sessions cannot: peer connection, shared accountability, and the experience of being understood by others who are walking a similar path. At Campus Recovery Center, group is not filler — it is a structured, facilitated clinical experience.

Types of Groups

  • Process groups — Open discussion of current challenges, feelings, and interpersonal dynamics in a safe, moderated environment.
  • Psychoeducational groups — Structured teaching on topics like the neuroscience of addiction, relapse warning signs, healthy communication, and boundary setting.
  • Skills-based groups — Hands-on practice with DBT skills, coping techniques, stress management, and mindfulness exercises.
  • Trauma groups — Specialized sessions for processing traumatic experiences in a confidential, peer-supported setting.
  • Relapse prevention groups — Identifying personal triggers, building a relapse prevention plan, and practicing real-world application of recovery skills.

Group Structure

Groups consist of 12–15 clients and are led by trained facilitators. Each session runs 60–90 minutes. Attendance and participation are expected as part of your treatment plan. All group content is confidential.

Psychiatric & Medical Services

Mental health and addiction are medical conditions that often require medical intervention alongside therapy. Our psychiatric and medical services ensure that the biological components of your condition are properly assessed and treated.

Psychiatric Evaluation

Every client receives a comprehensive psychiatric evaluation by our psychiatric nurse practitioner within the first days of admission. This evaluation assesses for co-occurring mental health conditions, medication needs, and safety concerns. The result is an individualized medication protocol — or a clinical determination that medication is not indicated.

Ongoing Medication Management

For clients on psychiatric medications, our provider conducts regular follow-up appointments to monitor effectiveness, adjust dosages, assess side effects, and ensure medications are working in coordination with your therapeutic goals. Medication management is never isolated from therapy — your therapist and psychiatric provider communicate regularly.

Medical Assessments

All clients receive a medical intake including health history review, physical assessment, and screening for infectious disease, nutritional deficiencies, and other health concerns that may affect treatment and recovery.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

For clients with opioid or alcohol use disorders, MAT can significantly reduce cravings, prevent relapse, and improve treatment retention. MAT is always used in combination with therapy — never as a standalone intervention.

Available Medications

  • Vivitrol (injectable Naltrexone) — A monthly injection that blocks opioid receptors and reduces alcohol cravings. No abuse potential.
  • Oral Naltrexone — Daily oral medication with the same mechanism as Vivitrol. Appropriate for clients who prefer non-injectable options.
  • Suboxone (buprenorphine/naloxone) — For opioid use disorder management. Reduces withdrawal symptoms and cravings while blocking the effects of other opioids.

All MAT decisions are made by our medical team based on clinical assessment, patient history, and treatment goals. Medications are monitored monthly. Learn more about MAT on our What Is MAT? page.

Case Management

Recovery doesn't happen in a vacuum. Many clients arrive at treatment facing practical challenges that, if left unaddressed, can undermine even the best clinical work. Our dedicated case managers help bridge the gap between treatment and real life.

Services Include

  • Assistance obtaining identification documents (ID, Social Security card, birth certificate)
  • Court and legal documentation support
  • Benefits coordination (food stamps, Medicaid, disability)
  • Resume writing and employment readiness
  • Housing referrals and sober living coordination
  • Transportation planning
  • Connection to community resources and support organizations

Aftercare & Alumni Support

Treatment doesn't end at discharge. Our aftercare planning begins early in your program and includes:

  • A written aftercare plan developed collaboratively with your therapist and case manager
  • 12-Step or mutual-aid program connections (AA, NA, SMART Recovery)
  • Referrals to outpatient therapists, psychiatrists, and support groups in your home community
  • Alumni network access for ongoing peer support after graduation
  • Relapse prevention resources and crisis planning

Learn more about our approach to long-term recovery on our Aftercare Planning page.
