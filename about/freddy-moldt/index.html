About Freddy

Freddy Moldt serves as Clinical Director at Campus Recovery Center, overseeing all clinical programming and therapeutic services. With deep expertise in evidence-based addiction treatment modalities, Freddy ensures that every client receives individualized, high-quality care.

As Clinical Director, Freddy is responsible for the development, implementation, and continuous improvement of treatment protocols across all levels of care, including Partial Hospitalization (PHP), Intensive Outpatient (IOP), and Outpatient (OP) programs. His leadership ensures that clinical staff deliver consistent, compassionate, and effective treatment to every individual who walks through our doors.

Clinical Expertise

Freddy brings a comprehensive skill set in the most effective, research-backed treatment approaches used in modern addiction and mental health care. His areas of expertise include:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) — Helping clients identify and restructure negative thought patterns that contribute to substance use and mental health challenges.
  • Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) — Teaching distress tolerance, emotional regulation, mindfulness, and interpersonal effectiveness skills.
  • Motivational Interviewing — Using collaborative, client-centered techniques to strengthen motivation and commitment to change.
  • Trauma-Informed Care — Recognizing the impact of trauma on addiction and recovery, and integrating trauma-sensitive practices across all programming.
  • Dual Diagnosis Treatment — Addressing co-occurring substance use and mental health disorders simultaneously for lasting recovery outcomes.

Treatment Philosophy

Freddy believes that effective treatment begins with meeting each client where they are. Recovery is not one-size-fits-all, and neither is the care at Campus Recovery Center. Under Freddy's clinical leadership, the treatment team develops personalized plans that address the biological, psychological, and social dimensions of addiction and mental health.

His approach emphasizes building a strong therapeutic alliance, fostering resilience, and empowering clients with the skills they need to sustain long-term recovery. Freddy is committed to maintaining the highest clinical standards while creating a supportive, non-judgmental environment where healing can take place.

Clinical Leadership at Campus Recovery Center

As Clinical Director, Freddy oversees a multidisciplinary team of licensed therapists, certified addiction professionals, psychiatric providers, and case managers. His responsibilities include:

  • Program development — Designing and refining clinical curricula for PHP, IOP, and Outpatient programs to reflect current research and best practices.
  • Staff supervision — Providing clinical oversight, mentorship, and continuing education for all therapeutic staff. Freddy leads weekly case consultations and peer review sessions.
  • Treatment planning — Ensuring every client's treatment plan is individualized, measurable, and regularly updated based on clinical progress.
  • Quality assurance — Working closely with administration to maintain Joint Commission accreditation standards and implement data-driven improvements to clinical outcomes.
  • Crisis intervention — Overseeing clinical protocols for high-risk situations including relapse episodes, psychiatric emergencies, and risk assessments.

A Day in Clinical Programming

Under Freddy's direction, a typical day at Campus Recovery Center is structured to balance therapeutic intensity with skill-building and peer support:

  1. Morning check-in — Clients begin the day with a facilitated group to set intentions, assess mood, and identify goals for the day.
  2. Individual therapy — One-on-one sessions with an assigned primary therapist using modalities such as CBT, DBT, or EMDR as clinically appropriate.
  3. Psychoeducational groups — Structured sessions on topics like relapse prevention, coping skills, emotional regulation, and healthy relationships.
  4. Process groups — Peer-facilitated discussions where clients practice vulnerability, accountability, and interpersonal skills in a safe environment.
  5. Wellness activities — Mindfulness exercises, stress management techniques, and life skills development to support whole-person recovery.

Commitment to Evidence-Based Care

Freddy is deeply committed to staying current with the latest research in addiction science and mental health treatment. He regularly reviews clinical literature, attends professional conferences, and integrates new findings into Campus Recovery Center's programming. This commitment ensures that clients receive treatment grounded in the most effective approaches available — not outdated methods or one-size-fits-all protocols.

Under his leadership, Campus Recovery Center has become known as one of the most highly credentialed outpatient facilities in Palm Beach County, with a clinical team that includes specialists in behavioral health, expressive arts therapy, movement-based psychotherapy, and trauma-focused care.
