Treatment Programs

What types of treatment programs does Campus Recovery Center offer?

We offer three levels of outpatient care: Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP) — 7 days/week, 5 hours/day; Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP) — 6 days/week, 3 hours/day; and Outpatient Program (OP) — 2 days/week. All levels include individual therapy, group therapy, psychiatric care, medication management, and case management. Learn more about our services →

How long does addiction treatment last?

Treatment length varies based on individual needs and progress. PHP typically lasts 2–4 weeks, IOP lasts 4–8 weeks, and OP continues as needed. Your clinical team will work with you to determine the appropriate duration and adjust as your recovery progresses.

What is the difference between PHP and IOP?

PHP (Partial Hospitalization Program) is more intensive — 5 hours per day, 7 days per week — and is typically the first step after detox or residential treatment. IOP (Intensive Outpatient Program) is a step down — 3 hours per day, 6 days per week — providing continued structure while allowing more independence. Both include individual therapy, group therapy, and psychiatric care. See our full PHP vs. IOP comparison →

What conditions do you treat?

We treat substance use disorders (alcohol, opioids, stimulants, benzodiazepines, prescription drugs), mental health conditions (depression, anxiety, PTSD, bipolar disorder, schizoaffective disorder, schizophrenia), and co-occurring dual diagnosis conditions. SUD treatment → | Mental health treatment →

What is Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)?

MAT combines FDA-approved medications with counseling and behavioral therapies to treat substance use disorders. We offer Vivitrol (injectable naltrexone), oral naltrexone, and Suboxone for opioid and alcohol use disorders. Medications are prescribed based on medical necessity and monitored by our medical team. Admissions

What is the admissions process?

Our admissions process has three steps: (1) Connect — call us to discuss your needs; (2) Verify & Assess — we verify your insurance and conduct a clinical assessment; (3) Begin Treatment — visit our facility to complete intake and start your program. Treatment can often begin within 24–48 hours. Start the admissions process →

Do I need a referral to start treatment?

No referral is required. You, a family member, or another treatment provider can contact our admissions team directly at (877) 995-0102 to begin the process.

Can I tour the facility before starting treatment?

Yes. We welcome and encourage facility tours. Contact our team at (877) 995-0102 to schedule a visit so you can see our campus and meet our staff before beginning treatment.

Insurance & Cost

What insurance plans do you accept?

We accept most major insurance plans including Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, United Healthcare, Humana, TRICARE, Medicaid, Medicare, Ambetter, and Molina Healthcare. Our admissions team verifies your benefits quickly and confidentially. View all accepted insurance →

What if I can't afford treatment or don't have insurance?

Campus Recovery Center offers self-pay options and financial assistance for qualifying individuals. Don't let cost prevent you from getting help — contact our admissions team at (877) 995-0102 to discuss your options.

Family & Support

Can family members be involved in treatment?

Yes. We offer comprehensive family support services that educate and support families throughout their loved one's treatment. We recognize the vital role family plays in recovery and encourage family involvement in the treatment process. Family resources →

What happens after I complete treatment?

Upon graduation, you receive aftercare planning and ongoing support including alumni coordination, connections to local 12-step groups and community resources, and continued access to our alumni network. We also offer sober living options through our nearby recovery residence. Aftercare planning →

Privacy & Accreditation

Is my treatment information kept confidential?

Absolutely. Your treatment records are protected by HIPAA and 42 CFR Part 2, which provides additional federal protections specifically for substance use disorder treatment records. Your information cannot be disclosed without your written consent except in very limited circumstances. Read our HIPAA policy →

Is Campus Recovery Center accredited?

Yes. Campus Recovery Center is accredited by The Joint Commission (formerly JCAHO), earning the Gold Seal of Approval. This accreditation demonstrates our commitment to meeting rigorous quality, safety, and performance standards. Learn about our accreditation →
