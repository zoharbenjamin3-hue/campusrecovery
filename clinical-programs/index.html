Addiction & Mental Health Clinical Programs

Author: Daniel Touloumis
Editor: Devin Scheller, Director of Operations
Medical Reviewer: Sahil Talwar, PA
Published: August 1, 2024
Last Edited: March 29, 2026

Campus Recovery Center addresses mental health and addiction through two complementary pillars: clinical programs and an environment of care. Our clinical programs tackle the psychological components through evidence-based therapy, while our environment of care shapes the day-to-day experience—building new habits, healthy routines, and a strong social support system.

Our Philosophy

Recovery is not only about therapy sessions. It is about the entire experience: the setting, the community, the structure, and the behaviors practiced every day. Our campus is designed as a collaborative, learning-based environment rather than a sterile healthcare center. Clients attend support groups, develop life skills, find meaningful work, and rebuild social connections.

Levels of Care

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

Our most intensive outpatient level of care, typically following completion of stabilization at detox or a residential program.

  • Seven days of clinical programming (Monday through Sunday)
  • Five hours per day
  • Individual counseling, group counseling, medical and psychiatric care
  • Case management and ancillary services

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

Typically follows PHP, providing the structure and education necessary for independent living.

  • Begins with six days per week, three hours per day
  • Clinical hours reduce as the individual progresses (three to five days per week)
  • Schedules individualized from available programming days
  • Individual counseling, group counseling, medical and psychiatric care
  • Case management and ancillary services

Outpatient Program (OP)

Continued care as individuals progress to a self-directed recovery program.

  • Two days per week of clinical programming
  • One day of individual counseling (one hour)
  • One day of group counseling
  • Medical and psychiatric care, case management, and ancillary services

Clinical Services Across All Levels

Individual Therapy

One-hour confidential, private appointments with a primary therapist assigned for the entire duration of the program. Primary therapists are licensed, master's-level educated, or Certified Addiction Professional (CAP) credentialed.

Group Therapy

Sessions conducted by a group facilitator among 12–15 clients. All groups are confidential, private, and engaging. Topics include:

  • Relapse prevention and stress management
  • Psychoeducational topics and life skills
  • Belief systems and process groups
  • Trauma, depression, anxiety, substance use, and grief

Medical & Psychiatric Services

All clients receive health assessments and physicals from a medical professional, including evaluation for disease, harm risk, and nutrition. A psychiatric nurse practitioner evaluates individuals for mental health conditions and creates individualized medication protocols.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

Vivitrol (injection) or oral Naltrexone may be prescribed for opioid and alcohol use disorders. Suboxone is also available. Medications are monitored and delivered monthly.

Case Management

A dedicated case manager assists with food stamps, ID cards, court and legal documentation, resume writing, and other practical needs essential for rebuilding a stable life.

Ancillary Services

12-Step programming, connections to local community resources, and aftercare support to maintain long-term sobriety after graduation.
