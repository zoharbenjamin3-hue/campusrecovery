Is It Safe To Go To A Drug Rehab In Florida?

Examining Florida's regulatory transformation and what makes accredited treatment facilities safe choices for recovery.

Home › News & Updates › Is It Safe To Go To A Drug Rehab In Florida?
Author: Daniel Touloumis
Editor: Devin Scheller, Director of Operations
Medical Reviewer: Sahil Talwar, PA
Published: August 22, 2024
Last Edited: March 29, 2026

Florida has long been a destination for addiction treatment, drawing individuals from across the country seeking recovery in a warm, supportive environment. But questions about safety have understandably followed the industry's rapid growth. The short answer: yes, it is safe to go to a drug rehab in Florida—particularly since the state has implemented some of the most comprehensive addiction treatment regulations in the nation.

Florida House Bill 807: Landmark Legislation

Florida House Bill 807 represented a turning point for addiction treatment regulation in the state. This landmark legislation addressed the most pressing concerns in the industry, targeting:

  • Patient brokering — Criminalizing the practice of paying or receiving kickbacks for referring patients to treatment facilities.
  • Insurance fraud — Strengthening penalties for fraudulent billing practices that exploited individuals seeking treatment.
  • Sober home safety — Establishing standards for recovery residences to protect vulnerable individuals in early recovery.
  • Marketing practices — Regulating deceptive marketing that misrepresented treatment services or outcomes.

This legislation sent a clear message: Florida was serious about cleaning up the addiction treatment industry and protecting patients.

Regulatory Oversight Bodies

Multiple organizations now provide oversight for addiction treatment facilities in Florida:

Department of Children and Families (DCF)

The Florida DCF licenses and regulates substance abuse treatment providers in the state. Facilities must meet specific standards for staffing, safety, clinical practices, and patient rights to obtain and maintain licensure.

The Joint Commission

Facilities that pursue Joint Commission accreditation undergo rigorous evaluation against hundreds of quality and safety standards. Accredited facilities like Campus Recovery Center have earned the Gold Seal of Approval, demonstrating a commitment to excellence that goes beyond minimum regulatory requirements.

Florida Association of Recovery Residences (FARR)

FARR provides voluntary certification for sober living homes, establishing standards for management, safety, and ethical operations. FARR-certified homes give individuals and families confidence that the recovery residence meets established quality benchmarks.

Community Response and Advocacy

Florida communities have also taken proactive steps to support safe, effective addiction treatment:

  • Naloxone training — Widespread distribution and training in the use of naloxone (Narcan) for opioid overdose reversal among first responders and community members.
  • Public health emergency declaration — In 2017, Governor Rick Scott declared the opioid crisis a public health emergency, mobilizing state resources for prevention, treatment, and enforcement.
  • County litigation — Multiple Florida counties pursued legal action against opioid manufacturers and distributors, seeking accountability for their role in fueling the crisis.

Delray Beach: A Case Study

Delray Beach, Florida serves as a powerful example of how community-wide coordination can transform addiction treatment safety. Once home to over 120 treatment facilities with escalating overdose rates, the city implemented a comprehensive strategy that included outreach programs, sober home regulations, law enforcement task forces, and industry collaboration. The result was an approximately 60% reduction in overdoses within a single year.

How to Choose a Safe Rehab in Florida

When selecting a treatment facility, look for these indicators of quality and safety:

  • Accreditation — Joint Commission accreditation or equivalent demonstrates adherence to rigorous standards.
  • Licensure — Verify the facility is licensed by the Florida Department of Children and Families.
  • Credentialed staff — Look for licensed therapists, certified addiction professionals, and qualified medical professionals.
  • Evidence-based treatment — The facility should offer established, clinically proven treatment approaches.
  • Transparent practices — Reputable facilities are forthcoming about their services, costs, and treatment philosophy.
  • Continuum of care — Quality programs offer multiple levels of care to support individuals through different stages of recovery.

Conclusion

Florida has undergone a significant regulatory transformation, evolving from the challenges of rapid industry growth to becoming a model for other states in addiction treatment oversight. With landmark legislation, multiple layers of regulatory oversight, community-driven initiatives, and a growing number of accredited facilities, Florida today offers some of the most regulated and accountable addiction treatment options in the country.
