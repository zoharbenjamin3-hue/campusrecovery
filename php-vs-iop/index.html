PHP vs IOP: Understanding Levels of Care

Choosing the right level of outpatient treatment is a critical step in recovery. Learn how Partial Hospitalization and Intensive Outpatient programs compare so you can make an informed decision.

Home › Resources › PHP vs IOP
Author: Daniel Touloumis
Editor: Devin Scheller, Director of Operations
Medical Reviewer: Sahil Talwar, PA
Published: August 1, 2024
Last Edited: March 29, 2026

When seeking treatment for substance use disorder or co-occurring mental health conditions, understanding the different levels of outpatient care is essential. Two of the most common — and most frequently confused — options are the Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP) and the Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP). Both provide structured, evidence-based treatment while allowing clients to live at home or in a sober living residence, but they differ significantly in intensity, time commitment, and clinical focus.

What Is a Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)?

A Partial Hospitalization Program is the highest level of outpatient care available. Sometimes called “day treatment,” PHP provides a near-inpatient level of clinical support without requiring overnight stays in a facility. At Campus Recovery Center, our PHP operates 5–6 hours per day, 5 days per week, delivering a comprehensive and immersive treatment experience.

PHP is typically recommended for individuals who:

  • Have recently completed medical detox or residential treatment and need a structured step-down
  • Are experiencing acute mental health symptoms that require daily clinical oversight
  • Need medication stabilization under close medical supervision
  • Have co-occurring substance use and mental health disorders requiring integrated care
  • Require more support than traditional outpatient can provide but do not need 24-hour residential care

During PHP, clients participate in a full day of therapeutic programming that may include individual therapy, group therapy, psychiatric evaluations, medication management, psychoeducation workshops, and holistic wellness activities. The daily structure helps establish healthy routines and coping skills that form the foundation of lasting recovery.

What Is an Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)?

An Intensive Outpatient Program offers a meaningful step down from PHP while still providing robust clinical support. IOP is designed for individuals who have achieved enough stability to benefit from fewer treatment hours while continuing to build on the progress they have made. At Campus Recovery Center, our IOP meets 3–4 hours per day, 3–5 days per week.

IOP is generally a good fit for individuals who:

  • Have completed PHP and are ready to transition to a less intensive level of care
  • Have a stable living environment and some established support systems
  • Need ongoing therapeutic support while beginning to re-engage with work, school, or family obligations
  • Are motivated and capable of applying coping strategies learned in treatment to daily life
  • Do not require daily medical monitoring but still benefit from regular clinical contact

IOP sessions typically include group therapy, individual counseling, relapse prevention planning, life skills training, and continued psychiatric care. The reduced schedule allows clients to practice applying recovery skills in real-world settings while maintaining a therapeutic safety net.

Key Differences Between PHP and IOP

Understanding the distinctions between these two levels of care helps clients and families choose the option that best matches their current needs.

Time Commitment

PHP requires approximately 25–30 hours per week of treatment, while IOP typically involves 9–20 hours per week. This difference is significant for individuals who need to balance treatment with employment, education, or childcare responsibilities.

Clinical Intensity

PHP provides more frequent contact with psychiatrists, therapists, and medical professionals. Clients receive daily assessments and have immediate access to medication adjustments when needed. IOP, while still clinically rigorous, spaces these contacts over fewer sessions, placing greater responsibility on the client to self-manage between appointments.

Structure and Supervision

PHP offers a highly structured environment that closely mirrors the routine of residential treatment. This level of structure can be particularly valuable during early recovery when individuals are most vulnerable to relapse triggers. IOP offers more flexibility, making it better suited for those who have already developed foundational recovery skills.

Typical Placement in the Continuum of Care

In the standard continuum, clients often progress from detox to residential treatment, then step down to PHP, followed by IOP, and finally standard outpatient care. However, not every individual follows this exact path. Some may enter treatment at the PHP level if their clinical assessment indicates that residential care is not necessary, while others may begin directly in IOP if their condition allows.

Which Level of Care Is Right for You?

The appropriate level of care depends on several factors, including the severity of the substance use disorder, the presence of co-occurring mental health conditions, the stability of the individual’s home environment, their support system, and their prior treatment history. At Campus Recovery Center, our clinical team conducts a thorough assessment at admission to determine which level of care will provide the best foundation for recovery.

It is important to remember that stepping down from PHP to IOP is not a sign of reduced commitment — it is a sign of progress. Each transition represents growing independence and confidence in applying recovery principles to everyday life.

PHP and IOP at Campus Recovery Center

Campus Recovery Center in Lake Worth Beach, Florida, offers both PHP and IOP as part of our outpatient treatment continuum. Our programs are Joint Commission accredited and staffed by licensed clinicians, board-certified psychiatrists, and compassionate support professionals. We treat substance use disorders, mental health conditions, and dual diagnosis through an integrated approach that combines evidence-based therapies with personalized care planning.

Both our PHP and IOP include:

  • Individual therapy with licensed counselors
  • Process-oriented and psychoeducational group therapy
  • Psychiatric evaluation and medication management
  • Case management and discharge planning
  • Family involvement and support services
  • Access to Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) when clinically appropriate

We accept most major insurance plans and can verify your benefits quickly and confidentially. Whether you are stepping down from residential care or seeking structured outpatient treatment for the first time, our admissions team is available 24/7 to help you determine the level of care that best fits your needs.
