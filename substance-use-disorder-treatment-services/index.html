Campus Recovery Center provides comprehensive substance use disorder treatment for adults in Lake Worth Beach, Florida. Our evidence-based approach addresses the full spectrum of addiction through a continuum of outpatient care—from intensive programming to independent recovery support.

Substances We Treat

  • Alcohol use disorder
  • Opioid use disorder (heroin, fentanyl, prescription opioids)
  • Stimulant addiction (cocaine, methamphetamine)
  • Benzodiazepine dependence
  • Chronic relapse
  • Polysubstance use

Our Treatment Approach

Many of our clients also receive treatment for co-occurring mental health conditions such as anxiety, depression, PTSD, or personality disorders. By treating both the addiction and its underlying causes simultaneously, we significantly improve outcomes and reduce the risk of relapse.

Continuum of Care

PHP

Partial Hospitalization Program: 7 days/week, 5 hours/day of intensive clinical programming for individuals transitioning from higher levels of care.

IOP

Intensive Outpatient Program: 6 days/week, 3 hours/day with progressive step-down as recovery strengthens.

OP

Outpatient Program: 2 days/week of individual and group counseling for ongoing recovery maintenance.

SUD Treatment Services

Individual Counseling

One-on-one sessions with experienced, compassionate clinicians who understand addiction. Your primary therapist is assigned for the entire duration of your program, providing consistent, personalized support throughout your recovery journey.

Group Therapy

Structured group sessions addressing essential recovery topics including trauma, depression, life skills, anxiety, substance use, grief, relapse prevention, and stress management. Groups consist of 12–15 clients and are facilitated by trained professionals.

Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)

When medically appropriate, anti-craving medications help prevent relapse for individuals with opioid or alcohol use disorders. Available medications include Vivitrol, oral Naltrexone, and Suboxone, all monitored by our medical team.

Psychiatric Care

Psychiatric nurse practitioner evaluations ensure that co-occurring mental health conditions are properly diagnosed and treated alongside the substance use disorder. Medication protocols are individualized to each client's needs.

Case Management

Practical life support including assistance with identification documents, legal documentation, resume writing, food stamps, and other essential services for rebuilding stability in recovery.

Co-Occurring Disorders

Substance use disorders rarely occur in isolation. Research shows that nearly half of individuals with a substance use disorder also have a co-occurring mental health condition. Our clinical team is trained in dual-diagnosis treatment, addressing conditions such as:

  • Depression and anxiety disorders
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
  • Bipolar disorders
  • Personality disorders
  • Chronic pain conditions

Treating addiction without addressing the underlying mental health condition often leads to relapse. Our integrated approach means both conditions are treated simultaneously by the same clinical team — not referred out to separate providers. Learn more about our dual diagnosis treatment.

Understanding Substance Use Disorders

Substance use disorder (SUD) is a chronic, treatable medical condition — not a moral failing. It occurs when repeated use of alcohol or drugs causes clinically significant impairment, including health problems, disability, and failure to meet responsibilities at work, school, or home. The American Society of Addiction Medicine (ASAM) and the DSM-5 both recognize SUD as a diagnosable condition with specific criteria and evidence-based treatments.

At Campus Recovery Center, we approach addiction as a brain-based disorder that responds to structured, evidence-based intervention. Our treatment is built around three pillars:

  • Clinical therapy — Individual and group sessions using CBT, DBT, motivational interviewing, and trauma-focused approaches to address the psychological roots of addiction.
  • Medical support — Psychiatric evaluation, medication management, and MAT to stabilize brain chemistry and reduce cravings.
  • Life skills and reintegration — Case management, vocational support, and 12-step connections to rebuild a stable, substance-free life.

Who Is This Program For?

Our SUD treatment programs are designed for adults (18+) who:

  • Have completed detox or residential treatment and need structured outpatient support
  • Are struggling with active addiction but do not require medical detoxification
  • Have experienced relapse after previous treatment attempts
  • Need dual diagnosis care for addiction combined with mental health conditions
  • Are stepping down from a higher level of care at another facility
  • Are court-ordered or referred by a healthcare provider for outpatient treatment
